Tawa SCD Club News - 20 June 2024

Hello everyone

Another great night's dancing, and good attendance - 29 dancers - which meant we had to have a "ghost dancer" at times. We had a few visitors, who wanted to be prepared for Saturday's dance - Todd Foster, who used to dance at Tawa but now dances at Cap City; Pat Reesby, ditto; and Russell whose surname I don't know, but who is from Lower Hutt Club. It was our last preparation night for our Annual Dance. Catherine is very pleased with how well everyone manages all the dances, and trusts you will do brilliantly on the big night! 

Unfortunately the evening had a not-so-pleasant ending for Laura, who had her car interfered with while we were dancing. I have forwarded her email to those of you who were there tonight, in case you might have noticed anything suspicious.  


Tonight's dances
All of tonight's dances, except the first one, are on our Annual Dance programme.

  1. The Dancing Bees (R) - This was the noticeboard walking warm-up dance. It is on the programme of the Linden Annual Dance and also of the New Dancers' Celebration. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/1535/
  2. Napier's Index (J) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/10535/ Remember it is Left hand to your partner in the Balance in line, and Right hand to your corner, both times.
  3. The Flower of Glasgow (S) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/12941/
  4.  Toast to the Mousies (R) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/17459/ 
  5. Links with St Petersburg (J) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13588/
  6. Jean Martin of Aberdeen (S) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/12542/
  7. A Capital Jig (J) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13581/
  8. The Montgomeries' Rant (R) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/4641/


Our Annual Dance - only two more sleeps ... (or just one more sleep if you read this on Friday morning)
Saturday 22 June, 7:30 pm, Ngaio Town Hall. 
Hall set-up: For those who have volunteered to help - 4 pm on Saturday afternoon. 

Remember:
  • Please arrive a little early - say 7:15 pm - to welcome our guests
  • Paid-up Tawa Club members get in for free!
  • Please bring two plates of finger food
  • Bring your water bottle - there will be no water dispensers (but you can fill up in the kitchen if necessary!) 
  • Wear your name tag - the hanger will be near the money table
  • Make good use of the sanitiser - bottles will be on the money table, and on either side of the stage
  • Check your crib sheet (attached below) before the dance
  • Don't get up for a 3-star dance unless you know it well! Instead, enjoy watching it from the sidelines. 


Next week (Thursday 27 June)
  • Ngaio Midwinter Dinner and Dance - Khandallah Presbyterian Church Hall, Ganges Road, Khandallah. 
  • Arrive at 6:30 pm. Dinner is at 7 pm. Dancing starts at 8 pm. 
  • Music by Hilary and Jason. Dress to the "Christmas" theme if you can. 
  • There will be no dancing at Tawa on this night
  • I understand they have reached their maximum number for the dinner, but you can still come along and enjoy the dancing, from 8 pm. The usual $5 door charge will apply.
