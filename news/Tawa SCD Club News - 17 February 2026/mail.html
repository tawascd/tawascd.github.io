Tawa SCD Club News - 17 February 2026

Good evening 

We've been having some pretty wild weather the last few days, haven't we. I hope that you've all come out of it unscathed, and you have not had any damage to your properties. 

Welcome back afternoon tea
This is a reminder of our "Welcome back afternoon tea" at Caroline's this coming Sunday - 22 February, at 2:00-5:00 pm in Tawa. This will be a great opportunity to reconnect with fellow club members, and chat about how we've spent the summer break. Please bring a small plate of afternoon tea type goodies. 
Caroline would like to have some idea of how many are likely to come along, so please could you let me know whether or not you will be attending. ASAP would be nice. 

Afternoon  dancing
A reminder of these very popular sessions. They happen on Tuesday afternoons at 12:45-2:45, at the St John's Anglican Church Hall, in Bassett St, Johnsonville (above the roundabout). The cost is $5 at the door.
  • On the 2nd and 4th Tuesdays of the month, there will be sessions for experienced dancers - taught by a number of Wellington Region teachers, in blocks of four to six sessions each. The first such session wil be next Tuesday 24 February, and the teacher will be Gaye Collin.
  • The 3rd Tuesday of the month will be general sessions, to be taught by Catherine McCutcheon, and is aimed at dancers of intermediate level and up. The first session will be on Tuesday 17 March.
  • A new group - a walking dancing group - will be trialled this year, on the first Tuesday of the month (the hall has now become available to us). This is class is aimed at current and past dancers who are finding the rigours of dancing physically demanding, but want to keep on with social contacts and the mental stimulus of dancing. The session will involve dancing, but at walking pace only. No bouncing skipchange required! This class will be taught by Gaye Collin, and the first session will be on Tuesday 3 March. Gaye's message is attached below.


Club nights resume
Our club nights start up again on Thursday 5 March, at 7:30. As usual I will have the door open at 7:00, so you can come in early, help with moving tables and chairs out of the way, get your shoes on, and catch up with friends, and be ready to dance at 7:30. We will have supper. We look forward to seeing everyone back after the summer break!

AGM on 26 March
According to the club's Constitution, we have to give members three weeks' notice of the date of our AGM. It will take place on Thursday 26 March, during our club  night. 

Nominations for committee roles will be called for in the first few weeks after the club's re-start.

  • At our recent committee meeting, Jo announced that she will be moving away from Wellington later this year, so she will be standing down as President. We are very sorry to be losing her.
  • Denis has indicated his willingness to be nominated for President
  • Hilary and Desiree have indicated they are willing to continue in their roles as Treasurer and Secretary respectively, but are open to anyone else wanting to put their name forward for these roles.
  • Caroline wishes to stand down.
  • Colin and Helen are willing to remain as committee members.

We will be looking for two more comiittee members, so please talk to any of the committe, or ask me for a nomination form.
