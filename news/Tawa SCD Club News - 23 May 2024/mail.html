Tawa SCD Club News - 23 May 2024

Hello everyone

Did anyone notice the full moon out there tonight? Wasn't it beautiful! It was cold when we came out at the end of class, though - felt like it could be a frost ...

We had good numbers again - 25, so just over three sets, which allowed someone to sit out and catch their breath when they needed to. It's nice to know they haven't abandoned us. I hope they are all coming right, and we look forward to seeing them back at club again soon. 


Tonight's dances 
The preparations for our Annual Dance continue, with five of tonight's dances being from the dance programme. 

  1. Glen Etive (R) - This was our "noticeboard" walking warm-up dance. Nice and simple. Devised by Iain Boyd. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/8051/
  2. Napier's Index (J) Tawa - The dance was dedicated to Keith Napier (Australia) who for many years maintained a computerised index of SC dances. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/10535/ We will be doing this dance again in coming weeks.
  3. Monymusk (S)  Tawa - The name refers to a village north-west of Aberdeen. It's a very old dance, dating back to 1768. It's kind of 'boring', but as someone remarked, "it allowed the men and women of the day to ogle one another across the dance". https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/4646/ 
  4. The Kissing Bridge (R) Tawa - Devised by Wellingtonian Romaine Butterfield. The start is the same as in the first dance of the night (cast behind own lines and back; reel on opp sides). Then the bit that catches many people out - down the middle for 3 bars, change sides by lady dancing forward under the man's arm, and back up. Catherine had us practise this turn a couple of times. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/14977/ 
  5. The Lords of the Wind (R) - Devised by Iain Boyd, published in his book of dances, "The Lord of the Rings Collection". This dance includes "snake passes", which Catherine had us practise first. The dancing couple dances both snake passes with the same supporting couple, and the supporting couple has to be careful to finish it in their own place (don't "overcook" it). I think the diagram with the crib is quite helpful in this case. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/3957/ 
  6. The Flower of Glasgow (S) Tawa - We did this dance last week. It contains the "circulating Allemande" (https://youtu.be/G0X_3mrHTic) and finishes with an all-round strathspey poussette.  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/12941/ 
  7. Swiss Lassie (R) - This was this week's "brief only" dance. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7282/ 
  8. Best Set in the Hall (J) Tawa - Devised by Auckland dancer Helen Greenwood. It's most recognisable formation is "Set to corner and cast away", also know as "Peter White Reels" https://youtu.be/hz68Sb89J8I . This dance has become very popular in the last 10 or so years. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/12426/
