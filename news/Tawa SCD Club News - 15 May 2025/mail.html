Tawa SCD Club News - 15 May 2025

Hello everyone

We had a great practise of some of the dances on the programme of our upcoming Annual Dance, with Jason and Hilary providing the music. Having invited members of other clubs, and having hoped for a good turn out, it felt a little disappointing that we only had five visitors. We had cleared the whole floor, with all the tables out of the way - anticipating hordes of dancers! But we had just three and a bit sets, so we had a lot of room to dance tonight. Thank you to those people who came extra early to help shift the tables. And also for putting everything back at the end of the night. 

Next week we will do some of the remaining dances on the programme, and any from tonight that need a bit more work. 

John took some photos of The Knot in Miss Gibson's Strathspey.

Tawa SCD Club Annual Dance - Saturday 24 May  (only one more week to go!)
This will be our big night, on which we are hosting dancers from clubs around the region and beyond. We hope that as many of our members as possible will be attending. 

We will need members to pitch in to make it a success.
  • We will be setting up the Ngaio Town Hall from 4pm on the day. The committee will be doing this, but we can probably do with one or two more volunteers to help. Talk to me if you are available. It will involve putting out chairs, setting up tables in the kitchen ready for supper, putting up programmes, and decorating the front of the stage (Catherine will bring the stage drapes, and Colin will bring greenery from his garden). This should only take about half to 3/4 of an hour.
  • The dance will start at 7:30 pm, but as we are the hosts, please arrive by 7:15 to welcome our guests.
  • Please wear your name tags. I will bring the name tag hanger 
  • Paid-up Tawa members will get in for free, "in lieu" of their contribution of two plates of finger food for supper. 
  • John and I will be minding the money table. 
  • Jo and Caroline will be organising supper, and they will appreciate help from a few members to set out the supper tables and the fruit salad, and to pour cups of tea/coffee. And to help wash up afterwards. Please talk to Jo if you can help. 
  • Packing up and tidying the hall at the end - please lend a hand to put the chairs and tables away. The more hands, the quicker it is all done.  


Looking ahead for Tawa Club 
Absences
  • 31 May - 9 July - After our Annual Dance, Catherine will be away for six weeks. During her absence Elizabeth Ferguson will be teaching our club, with the exception of 12 June, when she's unavailable, and we will have Maureen Sullivan teaching us. 
  • 18 June - 31 July - Hilary will be away. Denis will take on her Treasurer's tasks (i.e. door charges) while she's away. 

Special nights 
  • Thursday 24 July - From 24 July, on our club nights, we will have four classes for beginners (24, 31 July, 7, 14 August). We will start advertising these in early July. Members will be asked to put the word out in their communities.The first hour of our club nights will be devoted to getting the new dancers up to speed. In consideration of the fact that existing members will be supporting the new dancers, and get slightly less dancing time, they will not be charged the usual door fee for those four weeks.
  • Saturday 2 August - Reels Class. This will be open to members of all clubs. It will be held at the Petone Bowling Club, which has a large hall that will take up to nine sets (we are hoping for a great response from new dancers around the region), and has a good floor. The cost will be $10, which will include afternoon tea. 
  • Thursday 11 September - Dessert and Dance Night. Members of other clubs will be invited to join us, and we will have live music. 
  • Thursday 20 November - Tawa Club's Final Night for 2025. Other clubs will be invited to join us, and we will have live music. 
  • Saturday 20 June 2026 - The proposed date for Tawa's Annual Dance next year. Date to be confirmed.
