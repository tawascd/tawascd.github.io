Tonight's dances 
  1. Come What May (J) - This was our walking warm-up dance. Catherine had written the description on the board - to practice reading cribs ... It's a really basic 2C dance, devised by Romaine Butterfield. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/17900/ 
  2. Reel On (R) - Devised by Roy Goldring. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13467/ It's on Manawatu Club's dance programme.
  3. The Wind on Loch Fyne (S) - A 3C dance, in a triangular set. It's important to maintain the triangle shape - the 1s in the middle facing down, the 2, on the left (what would normally be the ladies' side) facing diagonally up, and the 3s, on the right (what would normally be the men's side)  facing diagonally up. A clever dance, and quite popular judging from the number of videos on this link: https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7180/ 
  4. The Sailor (R) - This is on Tawa's Annual Dance programme. On oldie but a goodie. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/5794/ 
  5. A Trip to Bavaria (R) - It's been a tradition in recent years for this dance to be on Ngaio's Annual Dance programme. Perhaps Jason will bring his tuba again! https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/6712/ 
  6. Miss Eleanor (S) - On Tawa's programme. A gentle 3C strathspey, which includes a 3C bourrel. Devised by Ann Dix, for her granddaughter Eleanor. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/16526/ 
  7. Major Ian Stewart (J) - The name of the dance refers to Major John Charles Stewart of Fasnacloich (known as Ian) who is the husband of Mrs Isobel Stewart of Fasnacloich, one of the founders of the RSCDS. The dance includes a 'hello-goodbye setting', followed by a 'different' circle - circle for 3 bars (6 slip steps) then pivot and chase anti-clockwise, back to place. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/4106/ 
  8. Trip to Timber Ridge (R) - This is the final dance on Tawa's Annual Dance programme. It is fast-paced with great music. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/14903/
