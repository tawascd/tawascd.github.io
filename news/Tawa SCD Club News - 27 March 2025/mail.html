Tonight's dances 
Tonight's teaching theme was the "Ladies' Chain", as it occurred in several of the dances. There was also one "Men's Chain", a formation that does not get danced nearly as often as the Ladies' variety. 
  1. Scotch Mixer (R) - This was our walking warm-up. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/14809/ 
  2. Napier's Index (J) - This dance was dedicated to Keith Napier, who was an early creator of an index of SC dances. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/10535/ 
  3. The Paisley Weavers (S) - Before we danced this 3C strathspey, we practised the Ladies' Chain. This dance starts with two chords - the first chord is for the bow/curtsey to one's partner, on the second chord, the couple in 2nd place cross to the opposite side. The dance is cleverly devised so that after each time through, the new 2nd couple finishes on their opposite side. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13727/ 
  4. Mrs Stewart's Jig (J) - The dance is dedicated to Mrs Ysobel Stewart of Fasnacloich, one of the founders of the RSCDS (or SCDS, at the time). In this dance a Grand Chain is followed immediately by, and flows into, a Ladies' Chain.https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/4805/ 
  5. The Sailor (R) - A perennial favourite. It is on our Annual Dance programme. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/5794/ 
  6. Blue Butterfly (S) - A nice strathspey that 'flowed' very well. It finishes with an Espagnole. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/14909/ 
  7. Beach Dancer (R) -This is on our Annual Dance programme. Another dance with a Ladies' Chain. Devised by NZer Eddy West. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/11587/ 
  8. Gaelforce Wind (J) - This dance includes a 2C  Set and Link, and a Men's Chain. In the Men's Chain, the men change places Left hand in the S-shaped diagonal, while the ladies dance the little oval on the sides.   https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/695/ This is also on our Annual Dance programme.
