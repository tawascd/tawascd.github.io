Tawa SCD Club News - 17 July 2025

Hello everyone

A much better evening to go out tonight, not quite so cold, and no wind or rain. It was lovely to have Catherine back teaching, and very nice to have Elizabeth back to dance with us this time. Numbers are still quite low, just two sets, but we had a great night's dancing. Catherine is preparing us for some of the Annual Dances coming up in the next few weeks. 


Ngaio Dinner Dance - Next week, 24 July 
Next Thursday, there will be no dancing at Tawa, as we will be joining Ngaio Club for their annual Dinner Dance, at the Khandallah Presbyterian Church Hall (opposite the New World Supermarket). Arrival time is 6:30 pm, and dinner is at 7:00, dancing will start at 8:00. The limit of 40 places for dinner were quite quickly filled, but anyone who did not (or could not) book for dinner, is welcome to join in for the dancing from 8:00 pm. There will be live music, and the dances will be easy favourites. Dances will be walked once, if needed. The theme is "Flower Power", dress to the theme if you can. The flyer is attached again, below. 

Introductory Classes
These will start the following week, on 31 July. There will be four classes, 31 July, 7, 14, 21 August. After these, the new dancers will be invited to continue coming to club, and will hopefully join the club as members, eventually. The new dancers will be the focus for the first hour of the club nights, the second hour will be for the rest of us. As a concession to paid-up club members, they will not pay the door charge for those four nights. 

I was pleased that the posters and small flyers I brought along tonight were taken by people to hopefully post in their communities. If anyone who wasn't there tonight would like me to print some more for next week, please let me know. The wider we can spread the word, the better. The poster is attached again - to print or to forward to friends or colleagues. 


Last night's dances
All but two of last night's dances are on upcoming Annual Dance programmes 
  1. Centenary Jig (J) - Upper Hutt. This was our walking warm-up dance. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/21320/
  2. The Round Reel of Eight (R) - Waikanae. This is a square set dance. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/5713/ 
  3. Midsummer Common (S) - Upper Hutt, Lower Hutt and Waikanae. A lovely strathspey, quite popular, as you can tell from the number of viewers in the link, and from the fact it is on the programme of three upcoming Annual Dances. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/16524/
  4. Davy Nick Nack (R) - Waikanae. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/1578/
  5. Hooper's Jig (J) - Levin. Starts with a clap. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/2979/
  6. The Border Weavers (S) - We did this dance last week. It includes a "mirror Allemande". https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7353/ 
  7. Violynne (R) - Devised by Rod Downey for violinist Lynne Scott. The shape of the dance represents the shape of a violin. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/12567/ 
  8. Best Set in the Hall (J) - Levin. Devised by Auckland dancer Helen Greenwood. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/12426/
