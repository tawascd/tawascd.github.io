Tawa SCD Club News - 27 June 2024 - Take 2

Hello again everyone

Having just despatched the report on the Ngaio dinner dance, here is all the “regular’ newsletter stuff ...

The next two weeks
First of all, we wish Catherine a lovely restful holiday. 

While she’s away, Maureen S will be teaching us. Most of you will know that Maureen in preparing to sit her Unit 5 (final) exam for the RSCDS Teaching Certificate, and this opportunity to teach the club will be very useful for her. Let’s support her by all turning up to class. 

When Catherine returns - on 18 July
We hope to attract some new people to come and find out about the joys of Scottish country dancing. The plan is for Catherine to devote the first half hour to an hour to getting the new dancers up to speed for the next few weeks. We need you, our members, to spread the word to your friends, neighbours, colleagues and rellies, that we are encouraging new people to join us. Tell them about what a wonderful activity SCD is. Tell them that it is excellent exercise, indoors, especially now that winter has set in and outdoor exercise is not so appealing. Tell them it is great for mental stimulation too! And sociable - SCD dancers are the loveliest people! 

This can also be a new beginning for those people who have started with us, but who for one reason or another, had their SCD journey interrupted. Come back! You’ll be made very welcome! 


Region Advanced classes
These classes, on two Saturday afternoons, 1:00 - 4:00 pm, at St John’s Church Hall in Johnsonville will be taught by Debbie Roxburgh, teacher of the Glendarroch Club in Whanganui. Debbie is also an RSCDS examiner and an internationally renowned SCD teacher, who has taught at several Summer Schools at St Andrews, Scotland. She is also currently the President of the NZ Branch of the RSCDS. 

Now is the time to register for this class. It will help the organisers to plan if people register early. Register on the Region Branch website, here. A flyer for the class is attached.

Other upcoming events
It’s rather a pity that the Upper Hutt and Levin dances are on the sat & Sun of the same weekend. 

  • THIS Saturday 29 June, 1:30 - 4:30 pm – Musicians’ Matariki Weekend Tea Dance, Uniting Church Hall, 87 Oxford St, Levin. Many of NZ’s SCD musicians are gathering to share ideas and make music together. In the afternoon, dancers are invited to join them to dance to their massed bands … $10 and a small plate for afternoon tea. Poster attached.
  • Saturday 6 July, 7:30 pm – Linden Club Annual Dance, Ngaio Town Hall. Music by The Saltire Band (Mary McD & friends). Poster attached.
  • Saturday 20 July evening – Upper Hutt Club Annual Dance, 6 pm. Early start with light refreshments first. Dancing starts at 6:30 pm. Music from Lynne Scott. Heather Elder and Sam Berkahn. Programme attached.   
  • Sunday 21 July, 2 pm - 6 pm – Levin Club Annual Dance, Ōhau Hall, Muhunoa West Road, Ōhau (off SH1, about 7.5 km south of Levin). Music by Saltire Scottish Dance Band. Raffles. Programme and cribs were attached last week.  
  • Saturday 24 August, 7:30 pm – Johnsonville and Capital City Combined Annual Dance, Newtown School Hall. Music by Lynne Scott, Sharlene Penman and Richard Hardie. Poster attached.
  • Saturday 19 October, 7 - 11 pm – Shetland Viking Dance. Early booking is advised, as numbers are strictly limited. Poster below.
