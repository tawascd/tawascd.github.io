Tawa SCD Club News - 20 November 2025

Hello everyone

Well, didn't we have a wonderful night to end our Tawa Club dancing year on a high! We had six plus sets on the floor - three of our own members, and another nearly four sets of visitors from other clubs. The band - Jason, Hilary and Shane - sounded terrific, and I gather they really enjoyed playing for us. John recorded them, so they have a memento of the night. 

Catherine did us proud with her great programme and patient briefing - especially for those people who're not too sure of some of the dances. Everyone did really well, and had a fabulous time. 

Many thank yous are due:
  • To those who arrived early tonight to shift the tables out of the way, and to those who helped to put everything back again at the end of the night
  • To Tawa members for bringing along their supper contributions
  • To Patricia, Helen C and Alison for setting out the supper, serving tea and coffee, and washing up afterwards (and John for helping with the washing up too)
  • To our guests for joining us for our final night
  • To everyone for their Koha contributions. Hilary has not yet told me how much we collected to donate to the Wellington Food Bank, but I will let you know in the next newsletter. 
  • To our fabulous musicians - Jason, Hilary and Shane
  • To John for being our photographer again - see below
  • And finally, and especially, to Catherine for her great programme.

Final events for 2025
But it's not quite the end of dancing for 2025. We have a few more events to go to:
  • This Sunday 23 November, 2-5pm - Our potluck afternoon tea at Hilary's home in Aotea. Please bring a small plate of fingerfood to share for afternoon tea. Also bring your dancing shoes if you wish to dance, or you may want to dance in bare feet on the grass (weather permitting). Jason will be attending and he and Hilary will play for us.  So far I have 21 people who have said they will attend. Let me know if you want to join us!
  • Monday 24 November, 7:30 pm - Johnsonville Club's Final and Tartan Night, Khandallah Town Hall. Everyone is welcome. Wear some tartan if you can. Most  dances will be walked. The programme and cribsheet are attached below. 
  • Saturday 29 November, 7:30 pm - Carterton SCD Club 25th Anniversary Ball, Wairarapa Events Centre, Holloway Street, Carterton. The programme and cribsheet are below. 
  • Sunday 30 November, 11 am - St Andrews Day Brunch/Lunch. Join Carterton Club members at FarriersBar and Eatery, 4 Queen Street, Masterton. RSVP by 22 Nov, to Elaine Laidlaw, see poster below.

Finally, a sad note

 
      I am sad to report that I have received news that Pam Fletcher, who danced with us for a few years, has passed away. Some of you will remember Pam when she danced at Tawa and Johnsonville Clubs. She suffered a stroke in mid-2024, and when I spoke to her a few months ago, she was making good progress with her rehabilitation, and was expecting to come back to dancing in the new year. Sadly, this is not to be. She had a fall last Friday, suffered a serious head injury, and passed away on Monday. 
I have emailed Pam's husband Ross to express our condolences, and will be sending a sympathy card on behalf of the Tawa SCD Club.
Pam's funeral will be at 1 pm, on Tuesday, at the Lychgate in Johnsonville. 


And so, the final club night for Tawa SCD Club for 2025 has come and gone, ending a great year of dancing. But we still have a few events to look forward to, before it is time to say Merry Christmas, Happy New Year, and all that ...

See you at our afternoon tea "potluck" at Hilary's on Sunday afternoon!

Cheers
Desiree
