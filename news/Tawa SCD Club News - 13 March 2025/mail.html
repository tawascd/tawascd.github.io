Tawa SCD Club News - 13 March 2025

Hello everyone

We had another great evening's dancing  last night. We had four-plus sets - 34 people, including a couple of visitors.

Last night's dances
The first few dances were "teaching dances" in which Catherine revisited some of the formations. Originally intended for the 'new' dancers, who started dancing with us in August last year, but disappointingly, they have not (yet) returned. Still, we can all do with some revision. 
  1. The Old Man of Storr (R) - This was tonight's walking warm-up dance. It's a nice easy dance, and is the first dance on the programme for our Annual Dance in May. https://www.scottish-country-dancing-dictionary.com/dance-crib/old-man-of-storr.html The title refers to a rocky hill on the Isle of Skye. 
  2. The Mad Hatter (J) - Devised by Wellington teacher Iain Boyd. Before dancing this, Catherine had us practise "advance and retire, and back-to-back with corners". https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/4058/ 
  3. Miss Gibson's Strathspey (S) - We first practised "turn corner, partner, corner, partner", Catherine emphasised that a firm handhold was necessary to turn all the way round in 2 bars. Then we practised the "Knot", emphasising that it starts with half a RH turn, and only after that, the R hands go over into Allemande hold. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/4484/ 
  4. Joie de Vivre (J) -  This is the second dance on our Annual Dance programme  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7283/ 
  5. Violynne (R) - Devised by J'ville's teacher Rod Downey for Lynne Scott, who plays the violin. It is a teaching dance, to teach figs of 8 along the side, and reels of 3 along the side. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/12567/ 
  6. Saint Martin's Strathspey (S) - A 4C dance for 4 couples, so each couple only do it once. It features reels of 4 on the sides. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/5842/ 
  7. A Capital Jig (J) - The dance starts with "Set and rotate", which we practised a few times, beforehand. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13581/ The title refers to Harrisburg, the capital of Pennsylvania.

Sad news 
Some of our longer-standing members may have known, or have heard of Gary Morris, who sadly passed away on Monday 10 March. He was a significant figure in the SCD world, especially in Wellington where he danced and taught for many years. He devised a number of dances. He was one of the first NZ examiners of aspiring SCD teachers. His funeral was held yesterday, Wednesday 12 March. A full obituary and reminiscences are likely to be published in the next issues of Kiwi News and Harbour City Happenings.
