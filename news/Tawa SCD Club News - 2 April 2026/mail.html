Tawa SCD Club News - 2 April 2026

Hello everyone

Because of being the Thursday before Easter, I was not expecting a great turnout tonight, but we actually did quite well, with 19 people there, including Joanne and Glenys from Waikanae Club. Their club dances on a Friday, and since tomorrow is Good Friday, and they won't be dancing then, they decided to get their dancing quota for the week with us.

We had a lovely class with Maureen S teaching in Catherine's absence. Maureen is working on her Units 4 and 5 of the Teacher's Certificate, and as part of the course work, she has to submit a list of all the classes she has taught since passing Unit 3, presenting a detailed review of six hours' independent teaching. So tonight was one of these classes. 

She had an interesting selection of dances for us, with a theme of 'promenades' and 'reels'.


Tonight's dances
  1. Come Under my Plaidie (J) - This was our walking warm-up. Sets of two couples facing each other, around in a circle. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/1304/
  2. Two to One (J) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/6824/ This was the first of the dances with a promenade in it. 
  3. Hame came our Gudeman (S) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/2742/ This had a 3C progressive promenade in it, which finished with the 1s casting off into 2nd place and the 2s dancing up to the top. 
  4. The Captain's House (R) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/15177/ This has reels on the side, and half-reels on the diagonal. 
  5. Mairi's Wedding (R) - A very popular dance everywhere. This has half-reels of four on the diagonal  - the track of the 1s forming a 'daisy' shape, and passing left shoulder in the middle; And reels of three across the set.  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/4102/ 
  6. The Sauchie Haugh (S) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/5904/ Maureen started by teaching the Rondel. Details to remember: the 2s make the arch; or the men make the arch. The ladies are passed in front of the men; or the 1s are passed in front of the 2s. This dance finishes with an all round strathspey poussette. 
  7. The Tri Mariner (R) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/9770/ This clever dance starts with a "meanwhile" - the 1s set and cast, while the 2s wait for two bars; then the 2s set and cast, while the 3s wait for a further two bars; then the 3s set and cast on the spot. They all end up ready for a 3C progressive promenade. Devised by Ann Dix, who also devised Culla Bay and The Committee Meeting, which we danced last week. 
  8. Les Remparts de Seville (J) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/17133/ Includes six bars of "Hello-Goodbye setting" and a set and link for three.

Region classes
These classes are highly recommended for all dancers if they wish to improve their skills - and why wouldn't you want to improve your skills? And it's always a good way to learn when you're in a class where everyone is at the same level.  
The Basics and the Intermediate classes are held fortnightly, at 7:30 pm, on alternating Tuesdays, at the St David's Multicultural Hall, off Elizabeth St, Petone. They start in May. Registrations must be in a week before the classes start, as the class would have to be cancelled if there are not enough takers. So why not decide NOW that you will attend, and register right away! 

If you're a new, or relatively new, dancer (in your 1st or 2nd year), the Basics classes are ideal. The dates are Tuesdays 5 and 19 May, 2, 16 and 30 June. You should register here by 28 April. Read the flyer below for all the details of what the classes will focus on.  

If you've been dancing for two or more years, the Intermediate classes will be excellent for you. Even if you have attended the Intermediate classes before, there is always an opportunity to learn more and to improve your skills and style. The dates are Tuesdays 12 and 26 May, 9 and 23 June and 7 July. You should register here by 5 May. The flyer below provides details on the classes. 

The Advanced classes will happen in July, so more about them a bit closer to that time.

Finally ...
This Sunday is the end of daylight saving. It happens at 3am, so remember to put your clocks back by one hour before going to bed on Saturday - 3am becomes 2 am. Yay, we get an extra hour's sleep! The trick to know which is which: "Spring forward, Fall back" (Fall being the American version of autumn). So it will probably be dark when we arrive for dancing next week.
