Tawa SCD Club News - 19 June 2025

Hello everyone

Elizabeth was back to teach us tonight. We had just on two sets, with either John or Elizabeth dancing. A cold night, a long weekend, and several people away travelling - all account for low numbers. But we still had a lovely night's dancing, with several favourite dances that are on Linden's Annual Dance programme.  


Tonight's dances
A slight theme developed, of 'long casts' and Allemandes, that Elizabeth said was unintentional. 
  1. Warm ups - These were a bit different, a bit like line dancing, but done to SCD music.
  2. The Highland Lass (R) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/2918/ It starts with a 'long cast'.
  3. The Cranberry Tart (J) - A favourite dance in the Wellington Region. It is a fugue - meaning that in the first 16 bars, the lady waits for two bars, then does the same as the man two bars later. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/9157/  
  4. Ettrick Water (S) - A nice strathspey, devised by Roy Goldring, that we hadn't come across before. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/2008/ It finishes with an Allemande.
  5. Toast to the Mousies (R) - A favourite! It is on Linden's programme. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/17459/ The deviser was inspired by the music, which was composed by brilliant fiddler Keith Smith. Consequently, the violin/fiddle part is devilishly difficult to play, according to Hilary.   
  6. The Findlays' Jig (J) - Also devised by Roy Goldring. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/9634/ There is a 3C Allemande and there are two 'long casts'. 
  7. Wisp of Thistle (S) - Apparently this dance was devised after the Queen Mother once described some SC dancers as being "light as wisps of thistle".https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7204/.  This includes a 3C Allemande. It is on Linden's programme
  8. The Wind on the Heath (R) - Devised by Wellingtonian Iain Boyd. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/11617/ It finishes with a chain progression, which we practised separately first.

Ngaio Club Annual Dance
Ngaio's Annual dance was an afternoon event, which was very well received. There were eight sets, of which Tawa members provided two sets. Maureen Robson and Jan Bruce attended again. John took photos, of course. It now seems traditional for Ngaio to have Trip to Bavaria on the programme, and the Ngaio men were prepared with their 'Bavarian(?)' hats. This time, Jason did not play his tuba, as in past years, as he now plays the lead on accordion.

The men doff their hats during Trip to Bavaria.
