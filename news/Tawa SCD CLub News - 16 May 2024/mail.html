Tawa SCD CLub News - 16 May 2024

Hello everyone

Another cold night tonight, but the heater and the dancing soon had us warmed up. We had the 'magic number' tonight - 24, i.e. 4x 3C sets, or 3x 4C sets (with Catherine dancing too). It was nice to see Pat Reesby, who used to dance with us regularly, but now shares herself around different clubs. She's the lovely lady who organises the Afternoon Dancing sessions.

Catherine - who, by the way, sounds seriously overworked, with so many teaching gigs in the next few weeks - is starting to prepare us for the our own Annual Dance. Five of tonight's dances are on the Tawa programme. And a couple of tonight's dances are on the programme for the New Dancers' Celebration (NDC), which is not till October, but the organisers (Lower Hutt Club) have already provided teachers with the programme, so we're getting in early.   


Tonight's dances

  1. Crabbit Shona (J) - NDC - This was our easy, peasy walking warm-up dance. It's a 2C dance, and we danced (walked) it in 3C sets. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13259/
  2. Chased Lovers (J) - Tawa - Though it's been around since 2013, it was only published last year in Book 53. Before dancing it we had another look at "corners pass and turn". We danced this last week. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/16465/
  3. Athens of the North (S) - Tawa - The name refers to Edinburgh - it was nicknamed 'Athens of the North' because of the Scottish Enlightenment and the many neo-classical buildings, built in the 18th century. The dance was one of twelve dances celebrating 90 years of the RSCDS Edinburgh Branch. It includes "diagonal half rights and lefts".  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/15643/
  4. The Lass of Richmond Hill (R) - Tawa - A popular dance, which includes a "corners' chain". https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7795/
  5. The Barmkin (R) - NDC - A square set. Read the extra info about the meaning of "Barmkin". https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/413/ 
  6. The Flower of Glasgow (S) - Tawa - Before dancing this, we practised a "circulating Allemande" - a 3C Allemande, except that while the 1s and 3s dance a regular Allemande facing up, the 2s dance it facing down. And the dance finishes with an all-round strathspey poussette. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/12941/ 
  7. Da Rain Dancin' (R) - This was the "brief only" dance. It includes a variation on "Hello-Goodbye setting". https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/1492/ 
  8. Trip to Timber Ridge (R) - Tawa - This is the last dance on Tawa's annual dance programme. It is fast, but has great music. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/14903/

King's Birthday Weekend School in Raglan
We have received the following message from the organiser of the KBW School: 
     

A gentle reminder to all dancers that King's Birthday Weekend is coming soon! 

We have a great social weekend planned in the seaside town of Raglan and would love to share it with you. 

Three wonderful teachers in Catherine McCutcheon and Diane Bradshaw from Wellington and our own Jeanette Lauder will be taking classes on Saturday and Sunday.

Don't forget the Saturday Night Ball in Raglan Town Hall dancing to the Music of Bourach Mor and friends Lynne Scott and Glenice Saunders.

We end on Sunday evening by going into the country for a Ceilidh at the village of Te Uku.

There are many accommodation options available in Raglan at this time of year. Some of us are staying at the Raglan Holiday Park and hope we will be joined by dancing neighbours. Others are camping, staying at the Back Packers or B&B's all within easy walking distance of the town hall. 

Most of all we hope people will share time together exploring Raglan's eateries and catching up for light refreshments after a satisfying days dancing. 

Don't forget to register on our web page https://kbwe.waibopscd.org.nz . If computers are not for you please ask a computer whiz friend to do it for you.

We look forward to seeing you in Raglan.

Cheers

Wayne McConnel and the KBW Committee

Email: kbwe@waibopscd.org.nz

Last Saturday's Ngaio Club Annual Dance
It was a great night, with eight sets on the floor, an excellent programme of mostly familiar and relatively easy dances, and fantastic music. Twelve Tawa members were there, plus Hilary in the band, of course. Two of our life members were there too. Jan Bruce enjoyed listening to the music and watching the dancing. And it was great to see John Gregory, who now dances in the Ngaio Club was that is more convenient for him than Tawa. He seems to be well, and as far as I know, he danced every dance!
