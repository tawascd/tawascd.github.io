We had another great night's dancing tonight, with 25 people attending. Catherine surprised me with one of tonight's dances - more about that below. 

Tonight's dances
The teaching themes tonight were "corner pass and turn" and "hello-goodbye setting".
  1. Festival Interceltique (R) - The walking warm-up dance. In a circle, and changing to a new partner after each time through.  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13385/   
  2. EH3 7AF (J) - The name refers to the post code of 12 Coates Crescent, which is the headquarters of the RSCDS in Edinburgh. The first half of the code (EH3) shows that it is Edinburgh, and the letters in the second half refer to the initials of the postman whose round this was, when the postcodes were first introduced. This bit of information comes from Margaret, who hails from Edinburgh, and whose father was a postman there. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/9107/ 
  3. Holyrood Strathspey (S) - Before dancing this we practised "corner pass and turn". Catherine pointed out that this formation offers the opportunity for beautiful covering, whereby the dancing couple briefly follow the turning couple. This dance was devised by Mervyn Short, a British teacher, who is very tall (not short!), who taught the advanced low-impact class at the Cambridge Summer School in 2019/20, that Catherine, Hilary and I attended. He was a very exacting teacher!  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/17897/
  4. Joie de Vivre (J) - It's French for "Joy of Living". This dance is on our Annual Dance programme. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7283/
  5. Flowers of Edinburgh (R) - A very lively and flirty dance. It was published in Book 1, so presumably quite an old, traditional dance. The deviser is unknown. Read the Extra Info to find out what the Flowers actually refer to. This dance is also on our Annual Dance programme. The dance finishes with a poussette, which we practised separately first, making sure it was danced as a square. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/2250/
  6. Thanks for All the Dancing (S) - Catherine did not at first tell us the name of the dance. Only after we had done the reel and the 'corners pass and turn', did she ask me if I recognised it. Indeed, it did look familiar - it was the dance I devised for Maureen Robson when she retired after 38 years of teaching Tawa SCD Club. Hilary composed two tunes specially for this dance, "Our Teacher, Maureen" and "Redwood Hall", which she and Aileen play in the video. Catherine likes the dance because it has 'corners pass and turn' in it, as well as an 'Espagnole'. I included the latter, because I knew it was one of Maureen's favourite formations.  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/19771/ We presented the framed dance and music to Maureen on her last teaching day (copy attached below). Catherine helped in getting the terminology right in the description, and made the suggestion as to how we might present this. 
  7. Links with St Petersburg (R) - To begin with, we did an exercise to practise the 'hello-goodbye setting', in a circle. Officially this formation is called "set to corners and partner", but is more commonly known as "hello-goodbye setting". The dance starts with 'Set and link' twice. This is on our Annual Dance programme. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13588/

Region Classes
Registrations for Basics and Intermediate classes are now open, further information is available on the Wellington Region website. 

Region classes are highly recommended if you wish to improve your dancing skills. The different levels provide an opportunity for dancers to work on their skills alongside others at the same level. It is good to have a different teacher, as each teacher will have their own style and the special points they wish to get across to their class. The classes are also a great way to meet people from other clubs. Whether you are a beginner, intermediate or advanced dancer, there is a class for you! If you are in doubt as to which class would be suitable for you, have a chat to Catherine.
