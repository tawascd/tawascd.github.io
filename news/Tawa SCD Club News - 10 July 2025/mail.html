Tawa SCD Club News - 10 July 2025

Hello everyone

What a great night's dancing we had tonight. We had three sets, much better than last week! We were delighted to welcome back Catherine from her travels. She only arrived back this afternoon, had a couple of hours' sleep, then came along to dance with us. How is that for devotion to the cub! We were pleased to see several people whom we hadn't seen for a few weeks. Nice to have you back! And we had a couple of small visitors - Mary and Duncan brought their grandchildren along. Young Arthur did very well for several dances. 

Tonight was Elizabeth's last night of teaching us. Thank you so much, Elizabeth, for standing in for Catherine while she was away. This may have been your last teaching night, but we hope you'll join us again to dance with us.

Tonight's dances
  1. Warm-ups - The walking warm-up was another version of couples in a large circle. 
  2. The Captain's House (R) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/15177/
  3. The Wild Geese (J) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7147/
  4. The Border Weavers (S) - We started by practising a "mirror Allemande". Unfortunately, neither o f the two videos in this link are very good. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7353/ 
  5. Machine Without Wheels (J) - This dance was devised at TAC ( the Canadian version of Summer School), where a rental car got all its wheels stolen! The dance includes a helix, which we practised first. It also includes some unusual combinations of figures, and there was a lot of laughing as the sets fell apart apart (the wheels were falling off!). But in the end it was quite well done. Sadly there is no video in the link https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/15052/
  6. Cramond Bridge (R) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/1405/
  7. The Wind Dragon (R) - Devised by Wellingtonian Iain Boyd, the name refers to a paper kite, with a long tail. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/8112/
