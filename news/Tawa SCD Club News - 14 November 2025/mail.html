Tawa SCD Club News - 14 November 2025

Hello everyone

Well, what a terrific night’s we had to finish Tawa SCD Club’s 2024 dancing year on a high!  We had an impressive eight sets on the floor, four sets of our own members, and a further four from Ngaio and other clubs. It was great to see everyone having such a wonderful time, and enjoying the fabulous music from The Cranberry Tarts, a.k.a Aileen Logie and Hilary Ferral. 

Many thank yous are due: 
  • To those early arrivals tonight who helped to move all the tables and chairs out of the way, and to those that helped to put everything back at the end of the night
  • To Tawa members for bringing along their supper contributions
  • To Jo, Caroline and Alison for setting out the supper, serving tea and coffee, and washing up afterwards
  • To our guests, for coming along to our final night
  • To everyone for their Koha contributions. Hilary, with her Treasurer’s hat on, revealed that we have raised $365 for the Wellington Food Bank
  • To our superb musicians, Hilary and Aileen
  • To John for being court photographer yet again - see below. There are more photos, which I will ask Colin to put on the club's website (
  • And finally, and especially, to Catherine for her great programme, and her patience in taking some of the newer dancers through the trickier dances (like Scott Meikle!); AND for all her terrific teaching all through the year.

Next week, Thursday 21 November – Ngaio Club Tartan and Final Night
Though we have finished our club nights, there are a few more clubs’ final nights to go, the most important of which is next week’s Ngaio Club Final NIght, because we have been especially invited, in the same way that Ngaio members attended out final night tonight. It will be held at 7:30 pm, at the Khandallah Presbyterian Church Hall, in Ganges Road, Khandallah. The programme and crib sheet are attached below. As it will be a Tartan Night, it will be great if you can wear some tartan. The door charge will be $5, and dancing will be followed by supper. There will be live music by Aileen and Hilary, The Cranberry Tarts, who played for us tonight. Sadly, it will probably be the last time they will be performing together as The Cranberry Tarts, as  Aileen, after 15 years in NZ, will be returning to Scotland early next year. She will be sorely missed by all of us! 

Saturday 23 November - Tawa’s end-of-year Potluck Dinner  
This will be at John and Désirée's place in Churton Park. Come along anytime from 4:30pm. Bring your favourite dish to share, a drink if you wish, and your partner if he/she feels like coming along. We aim to eat at about 6pm, and hopefully, we will be able to do a bit of dancing on the deck between dinner and dessert. I put out a sheet tonight, for people to indicate whether they will be attending, and so far, I have 16 names (including John and myself). If you haven't put your name on the list, it’s not too late - if you want to come, just email me. I would like to have an idea of how many to expect, so I can make sure I have enough plates, bowls and cutlery.
