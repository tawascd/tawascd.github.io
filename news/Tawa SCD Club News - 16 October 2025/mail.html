Tawa SCD Club News - 16 October 2025

Hello everyone

A somewhat better night than last week, at least no rain, but it suddenly got cold again! We had not quite three sets tonight, bit of an awkward number, but it allowed some people to sit out, and have a rest ... 

The night's focus was on setting (pas de Basque), formations involving corners, and a bit of practice of Strathspey setting and travelling steps. 

Corners: When the dancing couple is in second place, their first corner is diagonally across on the opposite side, and their second corner is diagonally across on their left side. Imagine holding your arms spread out, your right hand points at your 1st corner, and your left hand points at your 2nd corner. Your corners will (almost) always be the opposite gender. 

Strathspey: For the setting step (sideways) the non-weight bearing foot is raised behind the other calf, touching the calf - not "hanging off the back". Remember: Catherine likes things tidy. 
For the travelling step, the non-weight bearing leg must not be lifted up (neither forward, nor hanging off the back). Slide it forward past the other leg during the hop, it almost touches the floor as it slides past.  


Last night's dances
Numbers 1-4 were for everyone. No 5 & 6 were for the more experienced. No 7 was suitable for all. 

  1. Crom Allt (R) - This was our waking warm-up dance. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/1426/  It included Adv & Ret to corners, and turning them (RH, then LH). 
  2. Good Hearted Glasgow (J) - This dance was devised during a campaign in Glasgow to provide easy and accessible forms of exercise. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/2604/ 
  3. John McAlpin (S) - Right hand and left hand alternate all the way through. The dance includes "turn corner, partner, corner, partner". This is on Carterton's programme. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/3323/ 
  4. The March Hare (R) - Iain Boyd's version (there are four dances with the same name). We did this last week. Includes set twice to 2st corner, then set 2x to second corner. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/4142/ 
  5. The Wild Geese (J) - Very popular dance. Includes 1s and 3s set advancing. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7147/ 
  6. Rimutaka Incline (R) - This dance is on the programme for Carterton's 25th Anniversary Dance. It includes diagonal R&L.    The description is on the cribsheet for that dance (attached below).  
  7. The Saint John River (S) - The formations describe the way the river flows, meandering down the valley, then goes under some bridges, whirlpools, and flows back upriver because of the incoming tide. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/5829/

Sad news
I received the sad news that long-time SC dancer Helen Simmers passed away last week. Helen was the partner of Tawa Life Member John Gregory. She had not danced for some years, and had been in very poor health for over a year. The funeral was held on Tuesday afternoon. I sent an email to those of our members who know John and may have known Helen, to advise them of her passing and of funeral details. 
Helen and John G used to dance in Kelburn Club, and more or less held the club together in its final years, as President, Secretary and everything else, until members' ages rose and membership dropped to a point where they had to close the club. Helen was a delightful, friendly and warm-hearted lady, and she will be missed by many. Needless to say, there were many SC dancers at the funeral. "About three sets", said John, as he thanked people for attending.
