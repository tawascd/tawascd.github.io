Tonight's dances
Numbers 1-5 were for everyone. The last three dances were for the more experienced.
  1. Fair enough (J) - This was our walking warm-up dance. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/16803/
  2. Ecclefechan Feline (J) - This dance is on the programme's of both the New Dancers' Celebration, and Glendarroch's Annual Dance.https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/12848/. Ecclefechan is a village in Dumfries and Galloway, in Scotland.
  3. Twa Sparkling Een (J) - Translates as "Two sparkling Eyes". Devised by Wellington teacher Iain Boyd. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/6793/ I think this was our new dancers' introduction to "teapots" (1s dance RH across (1L with 2s & 1M with 3s) & pass RSh to dance LH across with other couple).
  4. Reel Easy Strathspey (S) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13828/
  5. Try as we might (J) - Devised by Lower Hutt teacher Gaye Collin, as a teaching dance to teach reels across the set. Sorry, no crib or video available. 
  6. Sugar Candie (S) - Very popular, as you can tell from the number of videos on the link. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/6373/ It finishes with a reel on  the side with a 'different" entry. 
  7. Emmett's Chase (J) - Devised by Perth (Aus) teacher John Brenchley (who also devised Orpington Caledonians). https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/14363/  Very clever, in the first time through, the lady leads and the man follows and they finish on opposite sides; in the second time through, the man leads, lady follows, and finish back on own sid. 
  8. Society Piper (R) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/6177/

New Dancers Preparation classes - Tuesdays 23 and 30 September
While the New Dancers' Celebration on 4 October is a special event for all those new dancers who have started dancing in the past two years, it is a dance for everyone. The more experienced dancers will be attending to support the new dancers. So please, do all come along to this special night. There will be two preparation classes which will take new (and not-so-new) dancers through all of the dances on the programme, so that everyone will be comfortable and confident, and will have a wonderful time.

Sad news
Shirley Kalogeropoulos, a dancer from Waikanae Club, advised that her mother, Esther Rudd, passed away on 15 September, aged 98. Esther danced at Tawa Club in the mid- to late 1970s and was the club's secretary for some time. Our sympathies go to Shirley and her family.
