Tonight's dances
Tonight was the second of the Introductory Classes, and Catherine covered the same as last week, with a few more finer details, which will be useful for the rest of us too: Keeping one's feet in 1st position (i.e. 90 degree angle) when standing still or doing slip step step; or 3rd position (T-position) for skip change, and a hop at the start of the Skip change of step (which unfortunately, some of us older dancers, like me!, can no longer do consistently - alas!).

We made good time, so Catherine introduced an extra new dance for the beginners -"Summer Mondays" - a 3C dance in a 4C set - another new concept for them to digest.  

After supper, the beginners either stayed to watch or went home, while the rest of us danced a further two dances, both of which are on the programme for the Waikanae Dance on Saturday:
  • The Aviator (J) - It's a dance that tends to confuse me, so Catherine calls it "Desiree's favourite dance". I think I have got it sussed now, though. Here is the link to the dance, and I find this video the most useful, because of the colour-matched T-shirts, so it is clear which couples belong together, and you can see the pattern of the 1st couple. The supporting couples have to remember that they are doing full diagonal reels. 
  • The Garry Strathspey (S) - A 4C dance in a 4C set, and everyone dances (almost) all of the time. It includes Schottische setting steps, which we didn't have time to practise fully, but we all managed. In the link https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/2409/ the first video features members of the Upper Hutt Club dancing at a Highland Gathering in Harcourt Park, Upper Hutt.  
  • Sadly, we ran out of time to dance "Red House", which Catherine had planned. It is also on Waikanae's programme. I love it, but I gather she doesn't ...


Sad news
It is with great sadness that the Lower Hutt Club advised that their president, Janice Henson, passed away on Sunday afternoon. She was a Life Member of the club and has served on the region committee and in numerous other SCD roles over four decades. Her passing is a huge loss to the Wellington SCD community. Details of her obituary are here: Janice Henson Obituary
A service will be held at Christ Church, Eastern Hutt Road, Taita, Lower Hutt, on Friday 8 August 2025 at 1.30pm. As the church is very small, Lower Hutt members and other dancers will gather at the Massey Avenue Bowling Club (opposite Woburn Railway Station) to watch the livestream of the service. Wearing kilts or tartan will be appropriate. 
For those unable to attend in person, the livestream can be watched online at Janice Henson Funeral Livestream.

RSCDS Fees
RSCDS fees are due by 21 August. Hilary will email current members about this. Membership is not compulsory, but we would strongly recommend people join. If you would like to become a member, please contact Hilary . 

Most of Tawa's current members will know that by joining the RSCDS, you are supporting the SCD community through teacher training, dance books and music CDs, and major SCD events, like Summer Schools and region classes. You get a range of benefits, including $100 off your registration fee for this year's RSCDS Summer School in Christchurch.

Here are the 2025-26 RSCDS membership fees:
Adult Joint: $92 per person
Adult Joint Email: $84 per person
Adult Single: $117
Adult Single Email: $101
Young Adult (18-24): $83
Young Adult (18-24) Email: $67

"Email" means you will receive the NZ Branch newsletter (Kiwi News) by email rather than as a physical copy. "Joint" is for a shared membership where two people live at the same address.

Summer School - 28 December 2025 to 5 January 2026
Attending Summer School is one of the great SCD experiences. Not only do you get to dance a lot, and learn heaps, but you get to meet dancers from all over NZ and quite a few from overseas. This year it will be in Christchurch. If you are thinking of going, now is the time to register, as the deadline for the "early bird" registration is 31 August. After this, there will be a $200 surcharge. 

If you think it's an expensive lark, the Branch offers several scholarships for people with fewer than 5 years, or more than 6 years' dancing experience, young adults (aged 18-24), JAMs (Junior Associate Members), musicians, and teacher prep. Details can be found on the Branch website here. You have to be a member of the RSCDS to qualify, and applications must be submitted by 30 September.
