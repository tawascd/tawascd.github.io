Tawa SCD Club News - 6 March 2025

Hello everyone

Welcome back to our first club night for the year. It was lovely to see everyone back after the summer break. We had 31 people tonight - four sets, with Catherine dancing too. Brilliant! Good to see some people back whom we hadn't seen for some time in 2024. 

We had a great night's dancing, and finished up with supper - it being the first Thursday of the month, and our first night of the year. Thank you to Jo L and Caroline for organising supper tonight. This year we will be having supper - and sometimes Tartan nights - on the first Thursday of the month. To share the task of doing supper, we would love some of our members to offer to take a turn. 


Tonight's dances
  1. Crabbit Shona (J) -  This was the warm-up dance, which we walked, to get our muscles and brains back into dancing mode. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13259/ 
  2. The Auld Grey Cat (R) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/295/ . This dance includes an Allemande.
  3. The Silver Grey (S) - A nice 3C strathspeys for 3 couples by Roy Goldring. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7517/
  4. St Andrew's Fair (J) - Another Roy Goldring dance. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/5802/ 
  5. The Dancing Bees (R) - You will know by now that it's not about the buzzy insects, but about a couple, called Mr and Mrs Bee. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/1535/ One thing to remember in the reels in promenade hold: the person on the 'inside' of the loop, should use very small steps, as the person on the 'outside' has further to go, and needs to use longer steps. 
  6. Muse Cottage (S) - The title refers to the home of SCD pianist Muriel Johnstone and her husband Bill Zobel. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/8068/ Another dance by Roy Goldring. 
  7. De'il Amang the Tailors (R) - A perennial favourite to finish a great night's dancing. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/1582/

Afternoon Dancing
Here is a message from Pat Reesby, the organiser of the afternoon dance sessions. 

Daytime dancing on Tuesdays is looking good for 2025.

The sessions take place in St John's Church Hall, 18 Basset Road, Johnsonville, starting at 12.45 pm (it's best to arrive a bit earlier to catch up with friends and get your shoes on) and finishing around 2.45 pm. It's the church 'on the hill', a few minutes' walk from the Johnsonville train station and bus hub. There's usually plenty of parking.

Daytime dancing for more advanced/experienced dancers takes place on the second and fourth Tuesday of each month. This group started on February 25 and our first tutor this year is Gaye Collin. 

The group is for those keen to learn more complicated dances and formations, as well as improving technique and style, such as phrasing. 

The Tuesday 'general' sessions will resume on March 18. 

Catherine McCutcheon will again take the group. Her focus is on popular dances, including some from clubs' dance programmes as they come up. Dances are usually walked but the sessions are best suited for those who know the common formations, eg reels of three and four, allemande, grand chain, ladies' chain, double triangles and so on.

These 'general' sessions are held on the third Tuesday of each month ... and also on the fifth Tuesday when there is one ... which there is next month! 

Getting to know people from other clubs is a bonus, especially when you catch up with them later at a Saturday dance. Quite a few people attend both 'experienced' and 'general' sessions.

The door charge is $5. This goes towards the hire of the hall and a modest remuneration to the tutor and to local musicians who occasionally play for the general sessions.

You can contact Pat Reesby, pmreesby@gmail.com for more info - otherwise, just come along. If you're unsure of the level of dancing required, it's an idea to have a word with your club tutor.

Here's a link to the Tuesday afternoon page on the Wellington Region website: https://wellingtonscd.org.nz/clubs/daytime-group/
