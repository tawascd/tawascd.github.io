Tawa SCD Club News - 14 August 2025

Hello everyone

We had a great turn-out tonight, with 29 people dancing. We were delighted that all eight new dancers returned for the third of our four beginners' classes. It was lovely to have a visit from Andrea Wells, of the Lower Hutt Club, and to Linda and Ivan Roxburgh from Mosgiel, who were again travelling through Wellington on their way to visit family in Waiouru. They always time their visits so they can join us at club. Excellent planning! 
 
As a bonus, we had an unplanned supper, because there was another birthday to celebrate, and a birthday cake to consume - mine. Thank you to everyone for the happy birthday wishes, and to Jo and Caroline for organising the cuppa. And my apologies to Catherine, because the time taken for supper cut into her teaching time. 


Tonight's dancing
In the first section - for our new dancers - Catherine covered Strathspey steps (setting and travelling), Advance and Retire, Back-to-back, and Allemande, in both Strathspey and Reel tempo. We did four dances with the new dancers, and after supper the rest of us did another two dances (or rather, one and a half!)

  1. Happy to Meet (J) - This was the warm-up walking dance. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/2782/
  2. Summer Mondays (J) - We did this last week  with the new dancers. 
  3. Sandy O'er the Lea (S) - This dance introduced an Allemande in Strathspey time. Catherine slightly modified bars 17-24 (1s follow 2s down, then 2s follow 1s back up) to make it easier for the new dancers.  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/5889/
  4. Allemande to Go (R) - This introduced the Allemande in Reel time. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/16844/

After this we had supper and then the new dancers either watched or went home. 

  1. Toast to the Mousies (R) - This is on the programme for the Ashhurst Dance, on Saturday. It is fast becoming a favourite dance in the region. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/17459/
  2. Aging Gracefully (S) - This is also on Ashhurst's programme. We were running out of time, so we were only able to walk this dance in 3C sets, giving each couple the opportunity to walk it as 1st C. It has an unusual 16-bar progression after which the 1s and 2s are in progressed positions and on opposite sides. The dance finishes with an unusual version of a crossing reel to get everyone back to their own sides. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/14974/

Waikanae 50th Celebration Dance
Last Saturday's Waikanae Dance in the Paraparaumu Memorial Hall was a huge success. Despite the cold and wet weather, there were eight sets of dancers on the floor, with people having travelled from Wellington, the Hutt Valley, Levin, Palmerston North and Whanganui. The band, consisting of Jason Morris, Hilary Ferral, Jonny Warnes, and including for the first time, Pip Morris, Jason's daughter, on drums, provided the usual great music. They played for the walking warm-up, after which dancers were asked to line up for the Grand March, led by a piper. I was glad they didn't make us go outside to line up (it was cold and wet!) - we all lined up at the back of the hall behind Damon and Gaye Collin, while Roy South directed the couples to the left or right. 

A great programme of popular dances was briefed by Waikanae's three teachers taking turns - Joy Tracey, Annie Christie and Isobel Harris-Clarke. Part-way though the first half, Shirley Kalogeropoulos, standing in for the Waikanae president who was away overseas, read out a history of the Waikanae club, over the past 50 years. All around the walls were vignettes of the club's history. 

Tawa was well represented with nine members there, plus of course Joy and Hilary who were up on stage. I've only got one photo of the event (Joy may have more, but she hasn't posted them on Facebook yet), but here is a video of the "Round Reel of Eight" - taken by Pat Reesby. https://youtu.be/8ALyGqXiMoY

The band: Pip Morris, Jonny Warnes, Jason Morris and Hilary Ferral. 

That's all from me for this week. Have a lovely weekend, and a great week.
