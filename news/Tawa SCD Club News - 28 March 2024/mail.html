Tawa SCD Club News - 28 March 2024

Hello everyone

We had a lovely evening’s dancing tonight, with live music from Lynne, Mary and Cassandra, who will also be playing at the Saturday’s Easter dance. Sharlene was unable to play because of a family emergency, so Cassandra had to step in at the last minute. With very little time to practise, didn’t she do well, especially in “Toast to the Mousies” !

We had quite good numbers to begin with (27 dancers), but many left after the Notices, so we were left with just two sets.

AGM next week
Tawa Club’s AGM will be held during next week’s class (Thursday 4 April), at around 8 pm. We will have supper - tea/coffee and biscuits. The AGM should be quick and painless, so please don’t stay away because of it. Most of the existing committee (Brenda, Catherine, Désirée, Hilary, Colin and Jo L ) have agreed to continue in their roles. However Verity has decided to stand down. So we have room for one more committee member. Anyone? If you feel you would like to join the committee, please email me or Brenda (brenda.vale@outlook.co.nz), or talk to one of us, at next week’s class. It is not an onerous commitment - the main ‘busy’ time will be for our Annual dance in June.  
 
Brenda, Hilary and Catherine will be presenting their reports. Early next week, I will send you the Agenda for this AGM, the Minutes of the last AGM, and Brenda’s and Hilary’s reports. Please read them before the meeting - it will save time, and paper …


Money matters …
  • Annual Subscriptions: Another reminder that the club subscriptions are due by the end of March - i.e. this weekend. The fee for 2024 is $25, and can be paid into the Tawa Club account: 11-7200-0098294-011. Remember to state your name and "Club sub” in the references. 
  • I will be printing the 2024 name tags, next week, so get in quick!

Upcoming events
  • This Saturday 30 April, 7:30 pm - The Easter Saturday Dance, which is part of the Teachers’ Workshop. The one we have been preparing for, for the last few weeks. Catherine says there will be some60 teachers there, so there will be plenty of support for dancers who might be a bit overwhelmed by the slightly trickier dances. It will be held in the Hutt Intermediate School Hall, Lower Hutt. Details on the poster below. 
  • Monday 15 April, 7:30 pm - Johnsonville Club Tartan Night, Khandallah Town Hall. $5. Suitable for new dancers, most dances will be walked. Live music from Aileen, Hilary and Jason. Wear some tartan if you can (but don’t worry if you don't have any). Poster attached below.
  • Saturday 27 April, 1:00-4:30 pm - Historical RSCDS Dances. St John’s Church Hall, Johnsonville. This year is the centenary of the publication of the first RSCDS book of dances. This special afternoon will focus on dances from the first five books, published before 1930. Some interesting dances that are not danced very frequently these days. Details on the Region website https://wellingtonscd.org.nz/ and poster below.
  • Saturday 11 May, 7:30 pm - Ngaio Club Annual Dance, Ngaio Town Hall. This is the first of the club annual dances of the year. Mark it in your diary! Poster attached below.

Sad news
I received the following message from the Ngaio Club:
It is with much sadness that I must tell you of the passing of Betty Thom on 21 March 2024. Betty was a very active member of our Club, and was a life member. She has held the positions of President, Secretary and Treasurer, for many years at a time. She was also active on the committee for atleast 13 years. I understand she was a very kind person and very helpful to beginners. A service will be held at 11am on Wednesday 27 March at the Guardian Funeral Home, 4 Moorefield Road, Johnsonville.  Messages to the family may be left in Betty’s tribute page at www.tributes.co.nz.  The service will also be live streamed, please refer to the tribute page for details.

I forwarded this message to those Tawa members who have been dancing long enough to have known Betty.
