Hello everyone

Another great night's dancing. Have you noticed the new heaters? Gone is the somewhat unreliable gas heater, replaced by two heat pumps, and boy, were they pumping out the warm air! A cluster of people were gathering by one of them, relishing the warmth, before dancing started. 

We had good attendance, with three sets (plus one). Several regulars have sent apologies - either under the weather, or away on holiday. But we had a new dancer joining us. Welcome to Cameron, who started dancing at Linden Club this year, and is keen to dance twice a week. He did really well, even dancing Links to St Petersburg, which had two formations he had not yet come across. Let's hope he stays! 

Being the first Thursday of the month it was a Tartan night, and we had supper - a cuppa and biscuits - and an opportunity for chatting with our fellow members. I'm sorry I forgot to remind people about it (I must admit I forgot about it myself!), and so not a lot of tartan was on show. Better luck next time. Cameron looked good in his brand-new kilt, though! 

I had some bad news about Pam Fletcher. On Sunday, I had a call from Pam's husband Ross, to let me know that she had suffered a serious medical event a couple of days earlier, and was being assessed in Auckland. She was to be brought to Wellington Hospital later on Sunday. She will not be able to return to dancing for some weeks. I am sure you will all join me in wishing her well, and hoping for a smooth recovery.

Coming up at Tawa Club

Next week, 13 June, we will have a practice night for our Annual Dance (which is on 22 June). We will have live music from Hilary and Jason, and perhaps the third member of the band for the dance, Johnny Bourne, will join them too (Aileen Logie is currently overseas). Catherine has compiled a programme of some of the dances that we need further practice with. If any dances show up as needing yet more work, we will be able to address them the following Thursday (before the dance). Catherine will not be sending out a 'brief only' dance for next week. 

Saturday 22 June - Our Annual Dance. At Ngaio Town Hall. It starts at 7:30, but it will be nice if our members can arrive a bit earlier - say 7:15 - to welcome visitors from other clubs. We will be setting up the hall at 4pm. We have ten volunteers who will help with the setting up - putting out chairs, setting up the kitchen, decorating the front of the stage (Colin will bring greenery from his garden, and John will bring the fairy lights), putting up the programmes and the decorative tea towels. 

We do hope that as many of our members as possible will be attending. I am attaching the programme and the cribsheet provided by Catherine, as a reminder. You will also find a link to the programme, cribs, diagrams, and videos on the MyStrathspey website here. 

More details about the Annual Dance in next week's news email.

After the Annual Dance - Catherine will be off on a well deserved holiday, and Maureen has agreed to teach the club on 4 and 11 July. She will be a busy lady, as she will also be standing in for Rod at Johnsonville while he is away around that time. 

From 18 July - After Catherine comes back from her holiday, we would like to encourage new dancers to join us. The first hour of our club nights will be devoted to getting new dancers up to speed. Club members could put their thinking caps on as to family members, colleagues, or neighbours, who they think might enjoy learning SCD. These nights will also be good for earlier beginners who have had a disrupted start. 

Later in the year - We are planning a Dessert and Dance night, to which we will invite Ngaio Club, and a Reels class which will be open to dancers from all clubs. More about these closer to the time.
