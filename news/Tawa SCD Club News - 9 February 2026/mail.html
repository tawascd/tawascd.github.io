Tawa SCD Club News - 9 February 2026

Hello everyone

Happy New Year!  Well, perhaps the year is not so new anymore, with more than a month gone already, but I hope you have all had a good start to the new year.

Now that the new dancing year is about to start again, here are some events for your calendars.

This coming Tuesday 10 February, 7:00-8:45 pm -  The last of the Summer Dancing Nights, on  the lawn in front of the Old Government Buildings, 55 Lambton Quay. See poster, below.  Gold coin donation. Sadly the last two were cancelled because of poor weather. 

Sunday 22 February, 2:00-5:00 pm - Welcome Back Pot Luck afternoon tea. We are lucky to be hosted again by Caroline and John Sherborne, at 74 Bing Lucas Drive, Tawa. Everyone is welcome - it's a great opportunity for a catch up with friends, and chat about what we have been up to over the summer break ... 
This year, it will be an afternoon tea event, rather than dinner. We found that this worked well with our end-of-year pot luck last November, so we are hoping for a good turnout.  Please bring a small plate of afternoon tea goodies. 

Thursday 5 March, 7:30 pm - Our Club nights will be starting up again! Same time, same place  -  7:30 pm at the Tawa Bowling Club. We look forward to seeing you all back again, ready for another year of dance, music and fun. We will even have supper on the first night!

Thursday 26 March - We will have our AGM. As usual it will be held on a club night.It should be short and sweet, so don't stay away because of it.

  • A message has arrived from The Royal Scottish Country Dance Society for the attention of club members whether or not they are RSCDS members, since those who have chosen not to join might be interested in the webinar topic and have an input to make. It can be accessed here. 

  • The Otago-Southland Region Easter Weekend School, will be in Invercargill, 3-6 April. The link to the Registration form is here. 

  • I have received the newsletter of the Canterburty Region. It comes as an email, not a link, so if anyone is interested, please let me know, and I will forward it to you.


Local News

Afternoon dancing - on Tuesdays 12:45-2:45, at the St John's Church Hall in Johnsonville. 

The experienced group will be starting up again on 24 February, with Gaye Collin taking the sessions on the 2nd and 4th Tuesdays of the month until 12 May. Gaye has emailed all those who are on the usual list of attendees, explaining her plans. Her message is attached below.

Gaye is also suggesting a new class for walking dancers. This is aimed at dancers who are struggling to dance because of physical limitations. This will also be held at St John's church Hall, on the 1st Tuesday of the month (as this venue is now available for us to use on the 1st Tuesday). Gaye message is also attached below. 


Sad news 
John Terris died suddenly on 2 February at his home in Lower Hutt. John used to occasionally dance at Tawa Cub about eight or ten years ago. He was a member of the Lower Hutt Club. His funeral will be at St James Anglican Church on Woburn Road on Wednesday 11 February, at 2 pm. Gaye and Damon Collin will attend to represent the SCD community. He was a past Mayor of Lower Hutt and also a Member of Parliament. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Terris 


That's all for now folks! 
I look forward to seeing you at Caroline's for the Welcome Back pot luck afternoon tea, at 2pm, on Sunday 22 February!
