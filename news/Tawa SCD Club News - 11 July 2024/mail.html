Tawa SCD Club News - 11 July 2024

Hello everyone

Another cold night, and only two sets of dancers - school holidays, people travelling overseas, people not well, etc ... But we had a good evening's dancing anyway. 

A big thank you to Maureen for her teaching for the last two weeks, and for holding the fort while Catherine was away. 


Tonight's dances
  1. Virginia Reel (R) - This was the noticeboard walking warm-up dance. For bars 24-32, the instructions say "slip step down and back" but we just walked it.  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/8162/ This is a ceilidh dance - which can get very rowdy in a ceilidh setting!  
  2. Kamo Karousel (R) -  Devised by the late Eddy West, of Whangarei - Kamo is a suburb of Whangarei. Eddy West also devised  "Beach Dancer". Before dancing this, we practised "set and rotate for 3 couples".  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/12669/
  3. Asilomar Romantic (S) - A lovely strathspey which includes a diamond poussette, and hello-goodbye setting. Asilomar is a resort in California, where the San Francisco RSCDS runs an annual SCD weekend. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/3791/
  4. Proud and Saucy (R) - Before dancing this we practised a "Men's Chain" (like a Ladies' Chain, except the roles are reversed, and the men cross Left hand, instead of RH). Also unusually, there were two "set and link"one after the other, which threw us a bit. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/11661/.  Unfortunately there is no video. 
  5. Ladies and Gents (R) - This dance includes both a Ladies' Chain, and a Men's Chain. It required quick thinking, especially as the music was rather fast. But we managed OK! Again, no video available.  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/10482/
  6. Delvine Side (S) - An old dance, dating back to 1790.  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/1615/ A 3C strathspey, in a 4C set - it goes on forever! Maureen gave us the choice between this and City of Belfast. I'd have preferred the latter ...
  7. Flora's Fancy (J) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/16533/
  8. Scott Meikle (R) - Maureen gave us the voice between dancing the 'brief only' dance (Red House), and "something easy and well-known" Scott Meikle. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/10920/ Hopefully we can do Red House another time.

Recent events
Robert and Brenda attended the Musicians' Tea Dance in Levin, on Saturday afternoon, 29 June. Tawa member Joy Tracey was one of the MCs - along with Isobel Harris Clark. Both are currently teaching the Waikanae Club. Robert's report in the Johnsonville Club newsletter mentions "Various combinations of musicians played for us over the afternoon, by the end on the stage there were four fiddles, a viola, a keyboard, an accordion, a recorder and a set of drums, a truly impressive band who sounded amazing. We seldom get to enjoy dancing to such a large group of musicians". They seem to have had a thoroughly enjoyable afternoon's dancing. 

Maureen and Desiree attended Linden's Annual Dance last Saturday, 6 July. Also attending were several Tawa members who are also members at Linden: Anne, Colin, Karen, Patricia, Fiona, Margaret and Cameron. Duncan McD was there as well, as Mary and her "Saltire Band" were on stage providing the music - and very good they sounded too!
