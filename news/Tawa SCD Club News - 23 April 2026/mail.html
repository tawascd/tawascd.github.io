Tawa SCD Club News - 23 April 2026

Hello everyone

How did you all fare during the relentless rains in the last few days? I hope nobody suffered any damage to their properties. I must admit that I was relieved that Johnsonville had the wisdom to cancel their Tartan Night, ditto with the Tuesday afternoon dancing. It was good to be able to stay home in the warm and dry.

Catherine was sure we would get a big turnout tonight because "nobody has been dancing in the past week". But, no, unfortunately it was again fairly sparse. It was lucky that Glenys and Joanne from Waikanae Club joined us to get their "weekly dancing fix", so we managed to have two full sets. 

We had some easy dances as Catherine thought some of our newer dancers might have been there, but they weren't. Come back everyone! We want you there!


Tonight's dances
All but two of tonight's dances (Nos 1 and 5) are on the programme of our upcoming Annual Dance.
  1. The Dancing Bees (R ) - This was our walking warm-up dance. It is well known and we didn't need a walk-through first, we did it with just a briefing. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/1535/ Beware - there are two dances with the same name. We did the one devised by Roy Goldring. 
  2. Dame Margaret's Jig (J) - Devised by Romaine Butterfield for longstanding Kelburn and Ngaio dancer Dame Margaret Sparrow. There is no video available. (R&L;  cross, cast, 1/2 Fig 8; adv & ret with 1st corners, adv & ret with 2nd corners; circle.)
  3. The Golden Years (S) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/15402/ Again, beware - there are four dances with the same name! We spent a little while reviewing Set and Link for 3 couples. 
  4. Flight to Melbourne (R) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/11483/  We did this square set dance last week.
  5. The Kelloholm Jig (J) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/3418/ A bit more challenging. It has an "interrupted diagonal half R&L" (interrupted by setting, and a one and a half turn)  
  6. Miss Eleanor (S) - We did this last week. It has a 3C bourrel.  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/16526/ 
  7. John of Bon Accord (R) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/3326/ We did this last week. It has 4 progressive half reels of 3. We again spent quite a bit of time on it.  
  8. The Chequered Court (J ) - This will be the final dance on our Annual Dance programme. We didn't have time to dance it right through, but we managed to walk all couples, and dance it once. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/10787/
