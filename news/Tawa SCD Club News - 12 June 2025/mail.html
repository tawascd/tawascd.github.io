Tawa SCD Club News - 12 June 2025

Hello everyone

Tonight we had Maureen teaching us, as Elizabeth was not available, due to a prior engagement, but she will be back next week. We had just over two sets of dancers there tonight, and we enjoyed some interesting dances, some of which will be on Ngaio's and/or Linden's Annual Dance programmes.  


Tonight's dances
  1. Knit the Pocky (R) - This 2C dance was our walking warm-up dance. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/3541/ A pocky was a woollen fingerless glove.
  2. Noah's Ark (J) - "This one's for me", Maureen said. She had just taken a fancy to it. She started by getting us to practise the final eight bars, a reel of 4, danced on the diagonal. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/11372/ (Note: Ignore the first video - it has the dancing couple starting from 2nd place and crossing up between the top couple). Move on to the second video. 
  3. Memories of MaryAnn (S) - Ngaio. This had one Set & link for 3C, two half diagonal reels, and a Knot. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/14976/ 
  4. Best Set in the Hall (J) - Ngaio, Linden. A very popular, but somewhat tricky dance. It is on both the Ngaio and Linden programmes, and both show this dance with stars, meaning, "for experienced dancers". About half of us know this dance well, half didn't, and a few struggled, but we got there! Don't get up to dance it at an Annual Dance, if you're not sure of yourself ...
  5. The Missing Turn (R ) - This dance includes a "meanwhile", and two Set and Link for 3C. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/10376/ 
  6. The Robertson Rant (S) - Ngaio. An 80-bar Strathspey in a square set. It goes just once through. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/5637/ Before we danced this Maureen had us practise the Highland Schottische setting step.  
  7. City of Sterling Reel (R) - Ngaio. This dance has three stars on Ngaio's programme, so for experienced dancers only. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13270/  It is quite tricky. It starts with a snowball chain. For the middle 16 bars, you need to remember the sequence R, R, L and  L , L, R. Cross RSH, turn RH, pass LSh and cross LSh, turn LH, pass RSh. I have to admit, it took me a while to get the hang of it!
