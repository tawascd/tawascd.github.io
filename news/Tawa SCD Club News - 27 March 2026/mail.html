Tawa SCD Club News - 27 March 2026

Hello everyone

Sorry this is a bit later than usual. I've been feeling a bit out of sorts.

Well, we managed just over two and a bit sets. With 23 people there for the AGM, and some people not dancing. 

Last night's dances
  1. Good Hearted Glasgow (J) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/2604/
  2. Catch the Wind (R) - Devised by Romaine Butterfiled, very popular world-wide. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/1087/
  3. The Swan and the Tay (S) - The name refers to two rivers - The Swan flowing through Perth in Australia, and the Tay flowing through Perth in Scotland. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/14741/
  4. A Diamond for Belfast (J) - We did this dance last week. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13852/
  5. AGM
  6. The Committee Meeting (R) - Devised by Ann Dix, who also devised Culla Bay. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13139/
  7. The Shortfield Jig (J) - 40 bars, https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13690/ A challenging dance that Catherine is planning to teach at the Easter School in Invercargill, and again, we were her guinea pigs! 


The AGM
Thankfully the AGM was short and sweet - just 18 minutes! Here are the brief details:
  • Jo and Hilary's reports (President's and Treasurer's) had been circulated beforehand, so didn't need to be read out. Catherine read out her report. 
  • Jo briefly thanked various people for their committee work. 
  • Hilary commented on the fact that the club made a loss last year. While that is not ideal, we have back-up funds in a savings account, but we do need people to pay their membership subs and turn up for club nights, because that's what pays the bills! (hall rent and tutor).  
  • Jo stood down as President, and Denis Spurdle was elected as the new President
  • Hilary and Desiree remain as Treasurer and Secretary, respectively - although somewhat reluctantly in Desiree's case. If anyone would be prepared to step into my role, please email me. I can send you a 'job description'. 
  • Colin and Helen M continue on the committee; Caroline stood down from the committee
  • Two new committee members offered, and were duly elected: Anne Hickling and Annette Bronkhorst. Thank you to both for stepping up. 
  • Duncan McD expressed a pledge of thanks to Jo for her work as President.

Catherine commented that a club does need a committee - i.e. a number of people so that ideas, plans and concerns can be discussed as a group, rather than decisions being taken by just one or two people.
