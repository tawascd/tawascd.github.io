What we did in tonight's beginners session
We started with a walking warm-up dance, followed by the usual stretches and foot exercises. The walking dance - "The Post Chaise" - included a "half fig 8 across", around the couple above. 

The next dance - "The Quicksilver Reel" - was a good dance to learn Reels of three.
1-8 The 1s dance a fig 8 along the side around the 2s and 3s (going in and down) 
9-16 The 3s dance a fig 8 on the side around the 2s and 1s (going in and up)
17-24 The 2s dance a fig 8 along the side, going out and up around the 1s, then out and down around the 3s
25-32 1s and 2s and 3s dance a reel of three - dancing the exact same path that they danced when doing the individual figs of 8. With the 1s going in and down, the 2s going out and up, and the 3s going in and up. https://youtu.be/OuWzbkrnyvE 
33-40 The 1s cross, cast off 1 place, while the 2s step up; then dance a half fig 8 around the 2s above them. https://youtu.be/R3kMWnkkSPg (note the video has 3 variations of cross, cast, half fig 8) 

Points to note: 
This is a 3C dance in a 4C set, so each couple dances the sequence twice.
It is a 40-bar dance, so there are five formations to remember. 
It includes a half fig 8 across

The third dance was "Short and very Sweet". More figs of 8 on the side (bars 1-8), flowing into reels of three on the side (bars 9-16). We danced it with a slight variation from the description: In bars 17-24, the 1s dance a full fig 8 across around the standing 2s https://youtu.be/0RG3VWOlMB0 (rather than 1s and 2s doing a double fig 8).  

Before dancing "12 Coates Crescent", we had a bit of step practise of strathspey setting step (sideways) and strathspey traveling step (forward). Catherine emphasised that the back foot should come through to the front smoothly, while remaining close to the floor - no jerking up of the knee, keeping the toes pointed down (not up or sideways!). https://youtu.be/ykCf1wmYETk Do look at the video and see how they move their feet! 

The final dance for the beginners was "Come What May". We practised dancing skip change of step backwards. It has the same rhythm as going forwards: Hop, step, close, step (with the step going back instead of forward). We practised "Advance and Retire" https://youtu.be/4fhjyKdydb and "Back-to-back" https://youtu.be/dnpQlzgnqXg In the dance both the Adv & Ret and the B-to-B are done on the diagonal. 


Tonight's dances 
The beginners danced the first five dances, the rest of us did the last two dances as well. 
  1. The Post Chaise (J) - This was our walking warm-up dance. There is no video, but there is a description on this link https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/9641/. We danced it in 3C sets. The difference is that in a 3C set, only the 3s, who are at the top dance bars 17-24.
  2. The Quicksilver Reel (J) - A simple dance to learn reels of three. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/10023/ 
  3. Short and Very Sweet (J) - Devised by Rod Downey, for the 90th birthday of Isla Norris, a very long-standing member of the Johnsonville Club, who was both "short and very sweet". https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/12568/ 
  4. 12 Coates Crescent (S) - We danced this last week. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/9106/ The name is the address of the HQ of the RSCDS in Edinburgh.
  5. Come What May (J) - We practised dancing backwards for "Advance and retire" and "Back-to-back". https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/17900/ 
  6. The Platinum Years (M) - This dance was devised by Lower Hutt teacher Chris Totton, to celebrate the 70th (Platinum) Anniversary of the Lower Hutt Club. Needless to say, it is on the programme of their 70th Anniversary Ball, on 7 September. It is 3C dance in a 4C set, and it is a medley, which means that in a 4C set, the dancing couple dances the sequence twice - once in strathspey tempo, and once in fast tempo. The description of the dance is attached below. 
  7. The Kilt Maker (R) - This dance describes the making of a kilt, including weaving the cloth and making the pleats. It was, as Catherine said, "A bit of fun". https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/3475/
