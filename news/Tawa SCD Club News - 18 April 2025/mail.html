Tawa SCD Club News - 18 April 2025

Good afternoon everyone

Happy Easter. I hope you are having a pleasant long weekend, despite the fairly miserable weather. A chance to veg out with a good book or the TV. I did that last night - a good, very gripping book on the go which I just had to finish - hence the tardiness of this week's newsletter.  

We had a lovely night's dancing last night. We were expecting a drop in attendance because of Easter, but as Catherine said, we had eight more people than she was expecting, including three visitors from other clubs. She had prepared a programme of dances with an Easter theme, and she wore her "Bunny Ears"! 

Last night's dances
  1. The White Rabbit (R) - This was our warm-up walking dance. Devised by Iain Boyd.  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7118/
  2. Round Reel of Eight (R) - An 88-bar reel in a square set. It requires first the ladies, then the men, to "put on their long legs" to chase all around the square set. As it is over in a flash, we did it twice. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/5713/ 
  3. Autumn Leaves (S) - Not exactly Easter-themed, but since it is autumn at Easter time in NZ, this will be appropriate. There are no fewer than five dances with the same title, but this is the one devised by Alec Hay. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/316/
  4. Blue Easter Bonnets (J) - A really 'easy, peasy' dance, often danced at the annual New Dancers' Celebration. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/588/
  5. Chopped Nuts and Chocolate Swirls (J) - It starts and ends with 3/4 R&L, and set, which represents the chopping of the nuts; The middle 16 bars, turn corners, then reel on the sides, represent the chocolate swirls.  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/14371/
  6. Anna Holden's Strathspey (S) - Not Easter-themed, but this lovely dance by John Drewry is on our Annual Dance programme. In July 2023, Catherine's general group of afternoon dancers recorded a video  of Anna Holden's Strathspey (once through) for the Pick-up 52 Project (a NZ Branch project to celebrate the centenary of the RSCDS). The video is the last on this link https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/159/ Note: Our late member Karen Harris is dancing as first lady, and this video was played at her memorial service.   
  7. Highland Rambler Easter Egg Hunter (R) - A 40-bar reel, by Roy Goldring. It includes two chases, similar to Catch the Wind.   https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/2924/
  8. Startled Rabbits (J) - (very experienced bunnies only) The extra info in the link states: "Dedicated to all those dancers for whom the next eight bars come as a complete surprise". This 5-couple jig caused more than a bit of confusion. I certainly felt like a startled rabbit! The 1s and 3s start at the same time. In bars 9-24, the 1s are involved in a double fig 8 first, then a reel, while the 3s are involved in a reel first, then a double fig 8. The people who find themselves in the middle position (the original 4s) are involved in the reel at the bottom first
  9. , then in the reel at the top. Confusion reigned! https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13615/
