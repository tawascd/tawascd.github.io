Tawa SCD Club News - 5 June 2025

Hello everyone

First of all, welcome and thank you to Elizabeth Ferguson for teaching us while Catherine is on holiday. Second, Happy Birthday to Elizabeth's husband Malcolm. It was lucky tonight was a Tartan night, with supper planned, which made for the perfect occasion to have a delicious birthday cake, made by Elizabeth. I asked her for the recipe, so perhaps I will be able to add that to next week's newsletter.

Numbers were a bit thin tonight, just over two sets. That's what happens when winter sets in - some people are off travelling, heading to warmer climes, and others perhaps are reluctant to venture out on such cold nights. Still, we had a lovely night's dancing. That's the advantage of braving the cold - the dancing soon warms you up! 


Tonight's dances
  1. Can Do Ceilidh (R) - The warm up walking dance.https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/19142/ Couples in a circle, Men with Ladies on their right. We did five repeats at walking pace, then danced it twice more In Reel time. 
  2. The Dancing Bees (R) - A popular dance devised by Roy Goldring. This is on Linden's Annual Dance programme. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/1535/ 
  3. A Capital Jig (J) - A great favourite. It starts with Set and Rotate, after which the 1s face up, ready for a RSH reel on the side. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13581/ 
  4. The Giant's Causeway (S) - This was a bit different! The set consisted of six people arranged in a hexagonal shape.https://www.scottish-country-dancing-dictionary.com/dance-crib/giants-causeway.html The name refers to the a geological formation along the northern coast of Northern Ireland. The basalt columns are mostly hexagonal shaped.
  5. Fahyda (R) - Devised by Melvyn Short. The music is a hornpipe. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13560/ 
  6. Triple Happiness (S) - This lovely strathspey is on Linden's programme. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/17122/ 
  7. Inchmickery (J) - We did this dance last week. It is also on Linden's Programme. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/9636/

Johnsonville Club's 70th Celebration
On King's Birthday Monday, Johnsonville club celebrated 70 years since its formation was a church group, which eventually became the Johnsonville SCD Club. The dance was held in the Khandallah Town Hall, where six or seven sets of dancers enjoyed dancing to the music of Jason Morris's band, consisting of Jason on accordion, Hilary on fiddle, Anna Miller on double bass, and Ian Telfar on drums. Many Lower Hutt Club members came along, instead of dancing in their own club (also usually dance on a Monday). 
A feature of the programme was the inclusion of several dances that Johnsonville's tutor (of 29 years!), Rod Downey, had devised to honour longstanding club members: Liz Rendell, John Markham, Isla and Eric Norris, John and Aline Homes, and Rod's wife Kristin Downey. The videos on these links were filmed by Pat Reesby, and John P was in attendance to film some more dances. He also took photos, of course, and recorded the music for the band.
