Tonights dances
Catherine was gentle on us for this first night - to ease our feet and brains back into dancing. We did a number of simple two-couple dances, which, after practising in twos, were danced in 3C sets.
  1. Welcome to the Dance (J) - The warm-up dance, to be walked. Groups of two couples facing each other, in a large circle, round the room. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7035/ Spot the difference with what we did tonight ...
  2. The Warratah Weaver (J) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/8203/ 
  3. The Auld Grey Cat (R) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/295/  This includes an Allemande, which we practised first.
  4. Sandy Oer the Lea (S) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/5889/ Another one with an Allemande
  5. Lady Catherine Bruces Reel (J) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/3594/ Includes an Allemande, and a Fig 8 across, around the standing couple above, which we practised. The lady needs to move smartly ahead so as not to block her partner. Two bars for each element.
  6. On the Quarter Deck (R) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/5080/ Finishes with Rights and Lefts. 
  7. The Paisley Weavers (S) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13727/ Starts with two chords, on the 2nd chord, the 2nd couple change sides. This includes a Grand chain, a Ladies Chain, and finishes with two half-R&Ls
  8. Deil Amang the Tailors (J) - Catherine gave us a choice of three dances (the other two were Scott Meikle, and Reel of the 51st Division). The popular choice (Scott Meikle) could not be done as it needs four couples, and there were not enough people left for full sets.
