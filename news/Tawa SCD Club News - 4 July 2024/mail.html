Tawa SCD Club News - 4 July 2024

Hello everyone

Brrr! We’re truly into winter now! A really cold night, thank goodness for the new heaters in the hall! The cold must have kept a few people cosily ensconced in their warm homes. We had only two five-couple sets tonight. Another reason that numbers were low is that some are sick, and quite a few people are away, probably seeking warmer climes. Still, dancing soon warmed us up, and we had a great night’s dancing, with Maureen teaching us in Catherine’s absence. And we had a couple of visitors – Russell from Lower Hutt, and Sharlene Penman, from Christchurch, who is in Wellington to play at Linden ’s Annual Dance on Saturday. 


Tonight’s dances
  1. Collie Law (J) – This was the noticeboard walking dance. A 2C dance, and as we had 5C sets, each dancing couple kept repeating it till they got to the bottom of the set. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/1285/
  2. General Stuart’s Reel (R) – https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/2428/ This dance includes “Hello-Goodbye” setting, with a different ending. 
  3. Ye Kent it Well (S) – Before dancing this, Maureen had us show her that we all knew how to dance a Ladies’ Chain. She was suitably impressed. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/10713/
  4. Miss Johnstone of Ardrossan (R) – a 5C Reel, devised by Roy Goldring, for Muriel Johnstone, the outstanding SCD pianist. A very popular dance, as you can see from the many videos on the link, and one of my faves. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/9638/ 
  5. A Jig for Robin (J) – This dance also had a Ladies’ Chain in it. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/3261/
  6. MacDonald of Keppoch (M) – This was a square set medley, which means it was danced through twice in Strathspey time, and then twice in Reel time. To my shame, I suffered not just a “senior moment” but a whole few “senior minutes”, while trying to figure out the transitions between the "reel of 4 across", to the "reel of 4 up and down". But in the end I got the hang of it! And medleys are always fun to dance! https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/16536/
  7. The Minister on the Loch (S) – A well-known favourite. It should be on everyone’s “must know” list. The minister in question, of course, refers to the famous painting “Reverend Robert Walker skating on Duddingston Loch”. Check out the extra info on the link. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/4411/
  8. The Aviator (R) – This was the “brief only” dance for the experienced. This relatively new dance is becoming increasingly popular (devised in 2011, published last year in Book 52). Everyone must have really swotted this one up for tonight, because the whole set “nailed it”! https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/14401/


Starting new dancers at Tawa …
From Thursday 18 July, when Catherine returns, we would like to have some new hopefuls joining us, to try out SCD. While people may not be so keen to start something new with beginners’ classes in February, with all the summery activities still being in full swing, Catherine thinks that we should promote SCD as a fun,  friendly, new indoors activity, in the winter time. Starting a  group of new dancers in winter has worked well for the Ngaio Club. 
We need YOUR help in inviting your friends, neighbours and rellies to come and try it out - from Thursday 18 July, at our usual time and place. The first half to an hour of each club night will be devoted to bringing new dancers up to speed with the basics. This may also be useful for those people who haven’t attended for some time, and may need a refresher. I will post some adverts on Facebook.
