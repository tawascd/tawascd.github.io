Tawa SCD Club News - 19 March 2026

Hello everyone

Turnout was a bit low tonight, just two sets. I guess some people are still away, making the most of the fine weather we've been having for the past week. Good to see we are getting some sort of summer after all. I do hope that the rising cost of petrol is not going to impact attendance in weeks to come. 

Despite the small numbers tonight, we had a great night's dancing, with some interesting dances, including one that was a bit - shall we say - challenging. With St Patrick's Day having been just two days go, Catherine had a programme with an Irish flavour for us.


Tonight's dances 
  1. The Fairy Ring (J) - This was our walking warm-up. Devised by Iain Boyd. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/2048/
  2. The Yorkshire Rose (J) - A recently published dance in Book 54.  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/16761/
  3. City of Belfast (S) - Great favourite, and we had to have this on an Irish night, didn't we? It finishes with an Espagnole, which needed a bit of a reminder for some. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13847/
  4. A Diamond for Belfast (J) - 40-bar, 3C dance in a 3C set. Unusually, this dance starts with a grand chain, and ends with 6 hands round and back. The extra info in the link gives some interesting details about Belfast's history (most of which I didn't know).  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13852/
  5. The Wild Geese (J) - An oldie but a goodie. The extra info in the link explains the reference to the Irish.  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7147/
  6. The Leprechaun (J) - Devised by Iain Boyd. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/3776/
  7. Glenoe (S) - The last 8 bars are a progressive grand chain, read the instructions in the crib. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/17905/
  8. Rye Twist (R) - This was quite challenging. Catherine intends to teach this at the Easter School in Invercargill, and we were her guinea pigs. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13163/

Last Tuesday was the first of the general goup's sessions. It was a special tribute to Pat Reesby, who is is relinquishing the role of Organiser, after having been in the role for six years. While dancers knew it would be Pat's tribute, it had been kept a secret from Pat. In her email to Elaine, she said "It was a surprise to arrive at St John's this afternoon and find that it was all about me! Not St Patrick's Day but St Patricia's Day".

Elaine presented Pat with a Thank You gift
