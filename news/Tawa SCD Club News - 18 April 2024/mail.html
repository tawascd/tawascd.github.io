Tonights dances
  1. Happy to Meet (J) - This was the warm-up walking dance, that Catherine had written up on the whiteboard for people to read and digest before dancing it. It is very simple dance devised by Iain Boyd, once to the bottom, i.e. each couple gets to dance it just once. 
  2. Maxwells Rant (R) This dance introduced the theme for tonight Mirror reels of 3 on opposite sides, https://youtu.be/XnT-2sPpFb8 in which the dancing couple cross the set in the first two bars, dancing through 2nd place on their opposite sides, finishing the reel in 1st position on opposite sides. The 2s and 3s dance a regular mirror reel. In this dance, for the 2nd reel, the 1s cross again, back to their own sides, finishing where they started. The 2s and 3s continue their regular mirror reels. Maxwells Rant is a very old dance (dating back to 1752) and is one of the must-know dances. You can tell how popular it is by the large number of videos on this link https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/4239/
  3. The Flowers of Edinburgh (R) This is also a very old dance, and is another of the must-know dances. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/2250/ Read the Extra info in the link, to see what the name of the dance refers to. Before dancing this we practised the Poussette. https://youtu.be/TEXJh0Sj-K0.  Points to remember about the poussette:
    1. It is done with 2 couples and it is a progression, i.e. couples finish in reversed places.
    2. It takes 8 bars of pas de Basque
    3. Youre either moving or turning, but never both at the same time. 
    4. It is a square formation - the person who is in the corner of the square, just pivots on the spot, while the other moves a quarter turn. 
    5. It needs firm arms, no spaghetti arms. The firmness comes from the muscles in the upper arm, not from a vice-like grip of the hands. Hands are joined at just slightly wider than shoulder width. 
    6. The jingle goes: Away from the centre; quarter turn; up or down; quarter turn; into the centre; half turn round; step back, step back. 
  4. The Halfway House, Petone (S) Devised by Elaine Laidlaw, teacher of the Carterton Club. The Halfway House refers to the Community House in Petone where the Region Committee used to meet, and which is halfway between Masterton (where Elaine lives) and Seatoun (where another committee member lives). https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/20736/ 
  5. Miss Johnstone of Ardrossan (R) Devised by Roy Goldring, for Muriel Johnstone, SCD pianist extraordinaire. This dance includes mirror reels of three on opposite sides.   https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/9638/ 
  6. A Capital Jig (J) This starts with Set and rotate, which we practised first. Another very popular dance. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13581/ 
  7. Saint John River  (S) We did this dance last week the one where the 1s are the meandering river, going under bridges and creating whirlpools, and the tide flows in and out, etc ... https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/5829/
  8. Liquid Assets (J) More "reels of 3 on opp sides. Devised by John Drewry, and in reference to the title, this dance flows like a tap (as do most of John Drewrys dances). https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/11524/

Sad news 
This week we heard of the passing on 9 April of former Tawa member and treasurer Jean Denne at the age of 83. Some of our longer-standing members will remember Jean as a keen and active member, and a meticulous treasurer. I first met Jean at the Johnsonville Club, and she was the person who encouraged me to also join Tawa Club. The Johnsonville SCD Clubs website has posted an obituary, which includes a number of photos of Jean at Johnsonville, taken by Loralee Hyde. https://www.johnsonvillescd.org.nz/2024/04/18/obituary-jean-denne/
