A lovely night's dancing, despite attendance being bit thin - just two 5C sets, including two visitors - Hilary's young granddaughter Briar, and Jason Morris. I wonder if the rising cost of petrol is having an effect?


Tonight's dances
  1. Joie de Vivre (J) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7283/ Warm-up walking dance.
  2. The Highland Rambler (R ) -A Roy Goldring dance https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/2924/
  3. Bruce's Men (S) - Another Goldring dance https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/11188/  Before dancing this, Catherine had us practise the Strathspey travelling step. She emphasised that there should be no "horse kicking back" nor "bike pedalling forward" of the non-hopping leg. The foot should remain close to the floor while bringing it through from the back to the front. Here's a detailed video clip of the strathspey travelling step. We also practised the diagonal half rights and lefts. 
  4. Swashbuckling Iain ( ) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/8577/
  5. Orpington Caledonians (R) - Very popular, devised by Australian John Brenchley. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/16523/
  6. The Belle of Bon Accord (S) - Devised by John Drewry. Bon Accord refers to the city of Aberdeen, where Drewry lived. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/462/
  7. The Montgomeries' Rant ( ) - An old dance dating back to 1749. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/4641/  One of my faves - the music is great, it helps you 'fly' across the set!


Region classes
I received the following email from Lesley Campbell, the Region Classes Coordinator, who wishes to advise that:
...the numbers registered for the Basics and Intermediate region classes are still very low. I know they are still a while away yet and people need to organise around other events.
However, we were also wondering if the fuel situation is putting people off travelling to the classes. If that's the case, do you think suggesting car-pooling for those interested in attending would be a good idea? Whatever you can do to encourage people to attend the classes would be greatly appreciated.  

Basic classes begin on 5 May (register by 28 April); Intermediate classes begin on 12 May (register by 5 May). Further details are in last week's newsletter, or check out the Region website. 
If you intend to go to any of these classes, please register ASAP. PlLease don't leave it till the last minute! And check if you can share rides with another dancer(s).

Stop press - Just to hand
Many dancers will be saddened to hear that Barry Skelton died peacefully in his sleep on Monday after several years of ill health. He was a prolific deviser, with more than 300 dances to his name, of which Pelorus Jack is the most famous, and is much loved around the SC dancing world. 
There will be an obituary in the next Kiwi News (which I am in the process of editing).
