We had a good turnout, with three sets. 


Tonight’s dances
  1. Trick or Treat (R) – This was the walking warm-up dance. Couple facing couple, in a circle. Circle 4hands round and back; 2 ladies adv & ret, then turn RH with one other – this is the “trick” as neither knows who the other will choose to turn, then 2 men turn RH with one other; all adv & ret, and all move forward to pass the other couple by the right, and face the next couple. 
  2. The Wild Geese (J) – We did this last week. It is on the programme of our final night. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7147/
  3. Oriel Strathspey (S) – This is one of the dances on the list of suggested dances for “Dance Around the World” day on Saturday 23 November, which just happens to be the day that we will be having our end-of-year potluck dinner. So we hope to be able to dance this on the deck (weather gods willing!) and John will video it so that we can send it to the RSCDS to be posted on their blog. The dance was devised by Ian Simmonds, who taught the Linden club for several decades. He also ran a group for  advanced dancers, who met in the garage of musician Peter Elmes, in Oriel Ave, in Tawa. By some wonderful coincidence, this house is now Jo L’s house! https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/5111/
  4. Hedwig’s Reel (R) – Another nod to Halloween (via Harry Potter …). https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13619/
  5. Pumpkin Pie (J) – https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/5390/
  6. Slytherin House (S) – Another reference to Harry Potter, and deliciously spooky music. It includes two “snake passes”. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/17460/
  7. Best Set in the Hall (J) – Devised by Auckland dancer Helen Greenwood, this is a NZ favourite. It includes the “Set to corner and cast away” formation. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/12426/ This dance is on our final night programme, but has a warning, that people should only dance this it if they know it. 
  8. Scott Meikle (R) - A 4C dance in a 4C set. As Catherine pointed out (and most of had never thought about it that way!), the 1s dance the first 8 bars with the 2s, then the next 8 bars with the 3s, Bars 17-24 involve everyone, and the last 8 bars the 1s dance with the 4s. Scott Meikle, for whom the dance was devised, is the son of well-know accordion player George Meikle. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/10920/ The dance is over in a flash, and we danced it twice, to two different tracks, the second one being George Meikle playing.

End-of-year activities for Tawa Club
  • Thursday 14 November, 7:30 pm - Tawa Club Final Night. Ngaio Club will be closed on this night, so that their members can join us for our final night. Members of other clubs may also attend. We will have live music from Aileen and Hilary, and we will be having supper - for which we will be asking our members to bring a plate of finger food to share. We will be donating the proceeds of the door charge to the Wellington Food Bank, as there is such a need for donations at this time of year. Instead of a door charge, we will be asking for “koha”, which means that the “10-trip” payments do not apply. So please remember to bring some cash on the night.

Next week, I will put out a list for people to indicate if they will be attending - partners are welcome too.

SUMMER SCHOOL
The latest Summer School newsletter is now available here (also attached below) . Do have a read, lots of interesting stuff is being offered. Make the most of Summer School being held in Wellington this year!  Keep track of what’s happening by following their Facebook posts  (You don’t have to be “on Facebook” to access this). 

It’s not too late to register as a commuter for the full school or for half the school. If you’re not able to attend the school, you might want to attend some or all of the evening events. A multi-pass to attend all evening events will cost $120 RSCDS, $150 Non RSCDS, $50 JAMs / $50 Juniors. Details of all the evening events are on the website too. 

  • Saturday 28 December – Opening night, MC Rod Downey
  • Sunday 29 December – Youth Night , MCs Isobel Harris Clark and Helen Oliver. This programme was devised and will be MD’d by some of our young dancers, and promises to be a very energetic night! Music by Wild Heather (Lynne, Mary McD and friends) 
  • Monday 30 December – Kiwiana Fancy Dress Night. MC Iain Matcham. This will be loads of fun. Start planning your Kiwiana costume now! The music will be provided by Aileen, Hilary and Jason.
                  

  • Tuesday 31 December – Hogmanay.  Note the late start, 8:00 pm, because it goes on till late 1:00 am! If you’ve never attended a Hogmanay dance, witnessed the Hogmanay ceremony, and danced or watched the 32-some Reel, now is your chance! The MCs are some of he Lower Hutt teachers.
  • Wednesday 1 January – A rest day! There will be no evening event, but the RSCDS NZ Branch will have its AGM at 2:00pm. 
  • Thursday 2 January – The President’s Ball. This will be quite a grand event, time to dress up in your best finery, and for the men, definitely a kilt (if you have one). The function starts with drinks and nibbles at 7:00 pm, during which the Branch President, Debbie Roxburgh, will try to meet as many of the people there as possible. Then before dancing starts, there will be a Grand March, which is a a sight to behold, and fun to take part in. This will also be “Tawa’s night”, as Catherine will be the MC. Also some of us will be helping on the door and in the kitchen. 
  • Friday 3 January – The Ceilidh. Not so much dancing (if any), more an evening for watching and enjoying the many items on show from some of our multi-talented school attendees. 
  • Saturday 4 January – Final Night. MC Elaine Laidlaw. The joyous conclusion of a great Summer School.
