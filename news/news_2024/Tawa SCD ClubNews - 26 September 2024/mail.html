Sad news
I have received the news that Edith Campbell passed away on 24 September. Edith was a very longstanding SC dancer and teacher, who has taught in the Wellington Region for 60 years. She and her late husband established the Seatoun Club, where she taught for 46 years. She was presented with a Region Appreciation Award at the Lower North Island Ball, in November last year. Edith wrote the column "The Spurtle Wielder" in every issue of Harbour City Happenings, since its inception in 1993. There will be a memorial service on 30 November (St Andrew's Day) at St Andrews on The Terrace. Time to be confirmed later. 


Tonight's dances
The new dancers did the first five dances, the rest of did the last three, all of which (and No. 5 as well) are on the programme of the Glendarroch dance on Saturday. 

  1. Happy to Meet (J) - This was the walking warm-up dance. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/2782/ 
  2. Try as we Might (R) - We did this dance last week. It includes reels across the dance. Sorry, no video available. 
  3. Reel Easy (R) - This was also dance last week, as it includes setting (Pas de Basque) which was new for Tanya and Hatsuhi. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13827/
  4. Clachna Cudain (S) - With this dance, Catherine introduced "Reels on opposite side", and "Reels on own side", also known as "Cross-over reels". https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/1219/ The dance finishes with an Allemande for 3 couples. 
  5. Catch the Wind (R) - This well-known dance, devised by Wellington teacher (now living in Otaki) Romaine Butterfield, is very popular all over the SCD world. It describes the courtship of two other Wellington teachers, Iain Boyd and Noelene O'Connor. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/1087/ 
  6. City Lights (J) - This dance introduced a new move, an "Allemande Turn", which is done by the corners in a formation similar to "Corners pass and turn". It is a fast-moving dance which flows very well. The name refers to the Charlie Chaplin film, "City Lights". https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/18408/
  7. Neidpath Castle (S) - This dance starts with Catherine's famous "meatball sandwich" - all set (slice of bread), all turn partner both hands twice (meatballs), all set (2nd slice of bread) ! Tonight we heard about a further definition: the grand chain is a plate, the figs of 8 on the sides are the spaghetti, and the two half-pousettes are the spiralling of the spaghetti on the fork. What wonderful imagination! Now you'll never forget how this dance goes ... Of course all of this has nothing to do with Neidpath Castle, which is an actual castle in the Borders of Scotland (read the extra info in the link) https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/4890/.  
  8. The Compleat Gardener (J) - A very busy dance that needs you to use "your long legs". https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/14552/ It flows very well. 



What was new for our new dancers?
For Tanya and Hatsuhi, who were not there last week, tonight's new elements were Reels across the set, and Pas de Basque. They managed both very well. The reels and steps were were explained in last week's email. 

Reels on Opposite sides, and reels on own sides. 
Also known as "cross-over reels", because the 1st couple cross to the other side to dance the reel.
These reels are similar to mirror reels, in that the "1s go in and down, 2s out and up, and 3s in and up", except that in this case, the 1s cross diagonally to pass through the place of the 2nd person on the opposite side, then completes the reel on the opposite side. They finish the reel in their partner's starting place. 
This is usually followed by "reels on own sides". The 1s are starting this second reel from their opposite sides (e.g. lady is in man's 1st position, and man is in lady's 1st position), and they cross back to their own sides to start the reel. https://youtu.be/XnT-2sPpFb8 In the video, watch the the path taken by the 1st lady (in the white top) and 1st man (in shorts).  

Allemande for 3 couples
We have done an Allemande for 2 couples. This takes three bars for the 1st couple to curve along the top to face down on the men's side. 
In the Allemande for 3 Couples, it takes just two bars for the 1s to get to face down on the men's side. They then have to take large steps down to make room for two couples coming down behind them (bars 3 & 4) . At the end of bar 4, all are facing down. 
All couples pivot to face into the set on bar 5, step into the centre (lady  under the man's arm) on bar 6, step back on bars 7 & 8.  
https://youtu.be/hF7Ug_5hk2c  The video show first a 2C Allemande, then a 3C Allemande. Count the bars for each, and see where the couples are at bars 2 and 3. 

Setting while turning away 
In Catch the Wind, the 1st couple set twice (i.e. 4 bars), but on the second setting (at bar 3), the lady turns by pulling back her right shoulder, ready to cast up.

Finally ...
This weekend marks the start of Daylight Saving. Don't forget to set your clocks/watches forward by one hour before you head off to bed on Saturday night. The switch happens at 2am on Sunday morning, when it changes to 3 am. You will lose an hour's sleep, but the good thing will be that it will be daylight when we arrive at club next week!
