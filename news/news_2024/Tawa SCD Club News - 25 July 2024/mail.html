Hello everyone

Last night was the second night of our beginners' series. Unfortunately none of the five people who came last week came back, but we had two new people come along - Tanya and Hatsuhi - and Murray a new dancer from Linden Club joined us too. We hope that some of last week's new people may come back next week. And we welcomed two visitors from Mosgiel - Ivan and Linda Roxburgh. 

As mentioned in last weeks' email, there would be no door charge tonight (or next week) for our members who have come along to support the new recruits. However, Hilary said that someone put $5 in the box anyway, but she doesn't know who that was. Would that person please email her, so she can keep her books straight ().

Catherine thanked the club members who had come along to support the new dancers. Hopefully everyone also got something extra out of it. It never hurts to repeat the basics.


Last night's dances
The early part of the evening was pretty much a repeat of last week's beginners' session. After step practise, we did three simple dances:
  • A Jig for Jeanie - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/9102/
  • Johnnie's New Reel - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/3343/
  • Summer Mondays - A simple dance Catherine devised as a teaching dance 

After the notices, the new people watched for a while, then went home. The rest of us danced three more dances:
  • Asilomar Romantic (S) - This is on Waikanae's Annual Dance programme. A lovely strathspey which includes a diamond poussette, and hello-goodbye setting. Asilomar is a resort in California, where the San Francisco RSCDS runs an annual SCD weekend. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/3791/. We did this dance two weeks ago, when Maureen was teaching us. 
  • The Galloping Carousel (J) - Also on Waikanae's programme. A simple 4C dance in a 4C set. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/2388/
  • Beach Dancer (J) - We finished with an old favourite. Devised by NZ dancer, the late Eddy West. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/11587/

For something a bit different: 
THIS Saturday 27 July, 6:30 pm  - Burns Night Supper. "A Scottish evening of food and Ceilidh with music, dance and poetry, celebrating the life of Ronnie Burns".  Winding Hall, 9 McKillop Street, Ranui, Porirua. Not an SCD event, this was brought to my attention by Patricia, whose nephew plays in the Wellington City Pipe Band. This event is presented by the Porirua Freemasons, the St Andrews Lodge No. 32. Tickets are $45 per person. Book in advance - Details on the poster below.

Summer School  2024-2025
As you will know by now, this year, Summer School will be hosted by the Wellington Region. Thi means that as Wellingtonians, we will have the choice between attending as "residents" (i.e. including accommodation at Scots College, and all meals), or as "commuters" (which includes classes and all evening events). As a commuter, you will save $1,100 on the normal cost of attending Summer School. This is a big opportunity for people who don't usually go to Summer School.

All details and registration are available on the Summer School website, here. Early Bird registrations for all options close 31 August, to avoid the late fee. Some scholarships to attend Summer School are being offered for various categories. Check out the RSCDS Branch website, here. 

There was an article about Summer School in the latest Harbour City Happenings, which detailed the special offers. FYI, I am copying the relevant part for you  here (HCH is attached also, in case you missed it):
 

We are working hard to make Summer School something all dancers can attend. 

We know that the New Year period is a family time and people often have other commitments. We also recognise that the cost of Summer School can be prohibitive for some.  To improve accessibility we are offering:

    1. Half-school attendance for commuters - From 28 Dec - 31 December, including Hogmanay, for $450. You can now choose this option in the registration form
    2. A discounted rate for a ticket to all evening functions, if paid in advance (The detail around this will be announced a bit later in the year)
    3. Casual lunches and dinners can be purchased if you are commuting (costs will be advised closer to the time)
    4. Areas at Scots College for commuters to hang out and relax or socialise between classes/activities

We are also looking into options for JAM (Junior dancers) classes, such as running a 3-day class.  Further information has been sent out to clubs and JAM families for feedback on this and other options.

The RSCDS NZ Branch offers a number of scholarships to attend the school.  Check out the Adult Scholarships and JAM scholarships on the Branch Website. Though it is not listed yet, the Branch Management Committee has advised that they are also going to offer a family scholarship. The closing date for all scholarship applications is 30 September 2024.
