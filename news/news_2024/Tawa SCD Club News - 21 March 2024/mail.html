Tonight’s dances
Again, Catherine has given us several dances that are on the Easter Saturday Dance programme. The first 5 dances  before the notices were all relatively straightforward. After the notices we had a couple that were a bit more involved - not difficult, but requiring quick thinking. 
  1. Diamond for Belfast (J ) - A 3C, 40-bar jig, which we walked, as a warm-up https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13852/ 
  2. It’s Nae Bother (J) - a 2C jig, that we danced in 3C sets. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/9087/
  3. The Braes of Bredalbane (S) - A very old dance, devised in 1795. It’s on the Easter Saturday Dance programme. Catherine was very pleased with how well it was danced by everyone. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/743/
  4. Reel Easy (R) - Before dancing this, we practised the Pas de Basque.  The dance starts with “set twice” - i.e two lots of two pas de Basque (setting once = one on the right, and one on the left). https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13827/ 
  5. The Wild Geese (J) - The title refers to Irish soldiers - check out the “extra info” in the link https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7147/
  6. Mr Graham W Donald (S) - We did this dance last week. It is on the Easter Saturday dance programme. Graham Donald is the UK teacher who will be running the Teachers Workshop over Easter. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/17463/ 
  7. Blackwater Reel (R) - This dance, as Catherine said, includes familiar formations - half R&L, “teapots” and a reel - but with a bit of a ’twist’. It requires quick thinking to keep it flowing. Very satisfying when it all goes well. This is also on Easter Saturday’s dance programme. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/9562/
