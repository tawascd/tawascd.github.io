Tawa SCD Club News - 10 October 2024

Hello everyone

Wow! Didn’t we have a great Dessert and Dance Night tonight! 

We started off with tea/coffee and a great spread of all sort of delicious dessert offerings. It was great to see so many Ngaio members come to join us for this special evening. Lots of chat and catching up with friends - what a great community the SCD world is! 

We had six sets on the floor, which was brilliant – Catherine was delighted. Tawa members were out in force, making up four of the sets, and our Ngaio visitors made up the other two sets. It was lovely to welcome Catherine’s sister Heather, and her other daughter Kirsten, both of whom are also excellent dancers. And it was great to see Margaret Sparrow, who used to be a very long-time and loyal member of Ngaio Club, but is no longer able to dance. She came to catch up with friends, watch the dancing, and enjoy the music. 

Thank you to Jo L and Caroline for being the queens of the kitchen, serving tea and coffee, and cleaning up when everyone had eaten their fill of dessert and started dancing. Jo has not been able to dance for the last four, five weeks, due to a badly sprained ankle, but she kindly came in to open up and to do the kitchen duties.

And then the dancing started, to live music from the band Wild Heather (minus one). We had Mary McD and Lynne Scott on fiddles, and Sharlene Penman on keyboard. Missing from the usual line-up was Anne-Marie Forsyth. 


Tonight’s dances

The names of the dances were mostly food related, and Catherine had made some great posters, each illustrating a dance – some needed a bit of a twist in thinking … All the dances were easy, and our beginners and Ngaio’s beginners all managed them very well.

  1. The Teddy Bears’ Picnic (J) – This was the walking warm-up dance. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/9896/
  2. Short and Very Sweet (J) – We have danced this a few times before https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/12568/
  3. 12 Coates Crescent (S) – The title refers to the street address of the RSCDS headquarters in Edinburgh. The poster showed a photo of the building and a somewhat cryptic item – a biscuit coated in (and dripping with) chocolate! https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/9106/
  4. Corn Rigs (R) – The poster showed the word “Rigs” crossed out, and the picture showed corn fritters! https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/1348/
  5. Golden Bracken (R) – The poster showed a tin of Golden Syrup. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/14339/
  6. The Cranberry Tart – Definitely dessert! In this dance, for the first 16 bars, the lady does the same moves as the man, but two bars later. Then there is a tandem reel across, followed by separate reels on the sides. The new dancers were advised that this dance was not suitable for them, and to just enjoy watching it. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/9157/
  7. Everything stops for Tea (S) – Related to the 1930s in London, when “everything did indeed stop for tea at 4pm”! The music the band played was suitably of the era. Do read the extra info in the link - it describes what each 8 bars represent.  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/8855/
  8. The Auld Grey Cat (R) – The poster showed a cup of Earl Grey Tea, and a cat - of course! https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/295/






Upcoming events
There are only two formal events left for this year. 

  • THIS Saturday 12 October, 7:30 pm -  The New Dancers’ Celebration. The biggest regional dancing event of the year! Hosted by the Lower Hutt and Upper Hutt Clubs, the venue is Knox Church Hall, High Street, Lower Hutt. This event celebrates all the dancers who have started on their SCD journey in the last two years, but the dance is for everyone - we are needed to support the new dancers! Admission:  RSCDS $12; Non-RSCDS $15; PLUS a plate of finger food for supper. People with a special invitation, i.e. dancers in their first two years of dancing, do not have to bring a plate for supper. In our club, that is just Laura. The programme and cribsheet are attached below.

More sad news
I received the following message from the Branch Secretary, Joy Dick:
Sadly I have another death notice to pass on.  It is for Ian Taylforth who died on Thursday 3rd October.  Ian held various positions in the Wellington Region; Region Committee member, Region Counsellor, Region President.  Ian danced at various clubs throughout his dancing life.  Ian's wife, Bronwyn Maysmor, died earlier this year.  Both of them were truly patient with, and gave lots of encouragement to, new dancers.  
A link to the Legacy obituary page is here - https://www.legacy.com/nz/obituaries/thepost-nz/name/ian-taylforth-obituary?id=56458463

Finally …
A big thank you to Catherine, for the great programme and posters; to Wild Heather for their fabulous music; to Jo and Caroline for their work in got kitchen; and for everyone for bringing in their plates of yummy dessert goodies.

Thank you also to the people who enquired how I was after my fall. I was only going to try to walk one or two dances tonight, but I walked them all except Corn Rigs, which had a poussette in it – I’m not game to do any bouncing yet. But I had a marvellous time, and after all that exercise, I am feeling better than I have felt all week! Dancing is definitely good for the body! Thanks everyone! 

Don’t forget to go to the New Dancers’ Celebration on Saturday! And bring a plate of finger food …(I don’t think I will be able to go - don’t want to push my luck)

See you next week
