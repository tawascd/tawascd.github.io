Tawa SCD Club News - 18 July 2024

Hello everyone

Welcome back to Catherine, she was in fine form  last night, despite feeling under the weather for a few days after she got back from her holiday ...

Last night was the first of a series of nights at which we encourage new people to come and try out SCD. We had five new people turn up, and I have had messages from a few more who couldn't make it tonight, but will come next week. This is really good news for the club - the more the merrier!  Let's hope we can provide the inspiration needed to get the new people to stay and enjoy SCD for a little longer! 

We have a change of plan with regard to the door charge for three weeks (including last night). Club members may come and dance for free for club nights July 18, 25, and August 1st. This reflects the arrangement that would have been in place for February beginner classes, and we feel that this is a fair way to proceed. It's always really helpful to people beginning their SCD journey to be amongst more experienced dancers, but we also need to recognise that there is less 'just dancing' to be done while new people are getting up to speed. 

We were a little thin on the ground last night - holidays, work, sickness and weather all playing a part. Do please come along if you can make it, especially over the next couple of weeks, to help get our new dancers happily launched. Bring an interested friend along too. It's not too late! 


Last night's dances
We started with a warm-up and stretches in a circle, and then Catherine introduced step practice. As the beginners will be getting a separate email with the details of what they learned, I won't go into too much detail here, but Catherine covered: slip step, skip change of step, RH and LH turns, 4 hands across and back, Fig 8 across, down the middle for 3 bars, up for 3 and cast for 2.

Then she put things together into 2 dances:
  • A Jig for Jeannie (J) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/9102/ (note: it is called a "Reel for Jeannie", but Catherine used it as a jig)
  • Johnnie's New Reel (R) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/3343/ (similarly, this is called "Johnnie's New Jig", but we danced it as a reel)

After the notices, the new people either went home or just watched while the rest of us danced:
  • Glasgow City Strathspey (S) - This dance is on the programme for Upper Hutt's dance. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/21324/ It starts with the 1s & 2s dancing the Knot, and the instructions for bars 9-32 look quite complicated in the crib, but they are actually like "Best Set in the Hall", except it's in Strathspey time.  
  • Jaki's Jamboree Jig (J) - This dance is on the Levin Dance programme. It was devised by Isobel Harris-Clark, who who started her dancing in the Levin Club, and who now does some of the teaching in Waikanae, along with Joy, and one other, Annie. The dance was devised for Jaki Williams, who has practically "held the Levin Club together", while they have been without a regular teacher for the last few years. As well as Jaki's long involvement with SCD, she was appointed a Member of the NZ order of Merit (MNZM) to recognise her considerable services to Scouting (which inspired the title of the dance). There is no video available. The instructions are in the crib sheet for the Levin Dance, provided by the Levin Club (attached below).
  • Deil amang the Tailors (R) - Just a quick finish with an easy, well-known dance. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/1582/
