Tawa SCD Club News - 24 October 2024

Hello everyone

We had a pretty good turn-out tonight with three sets, including some visitors from the Lower Hutt Club - Chris, Anna and Andrea. We were pleased to welcome back to Alison and Denis who have returned from their trip to Japan. 


Tonight's dances

  1. Jig to the Music (J) - This was the walking warm-up dance. Today, we did not first 'walk' the dance, to learn what was in it. Instead, Catherine just 'briefed' it, which is a new skill to be learned for our new dancers. Catherine mentioned that at dance events, dances are not usually 'walked' first - they are just 'briefed'. So dancers must listen and imagine the formations in their heads. This is a very simple dance. It is the first dance on the programme of the Carterton Annual Dance.  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13618/ 
  2. The Dancing Bees (R) - This dance was devised for a couple whose surname was Bee. It includes the 1s dancing a tandem reel of 3 across the dance, in promenade hold - first with the 2s, then with the 3s. The reel across the dance was new for the beginners, as was the petronella turn back to the sides. https://youtu.be/Ry5bvvyR0g4
  3. Seann Truibhas Willichan (S) - Before dancing this, we practised the travelling strathspey step (moving forward) and the strathspey setting step (moving sideways). Then we practised dancing travelling step for 2 bars, then setting step for 2 bars, and repeat. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/5982/ Do read the extra info, about the pronunciation of the name. The first video is very clear, and is one of the "covid lockdown" performances. 
  4. The Wild Geese (J) -This dance includes dancing pas de basque while moving forward.  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7147/ 
  5. The Rambling Pitchfork (R) -  This dance is on the Carterton Annual Dance programme. Before dancing this we had a quick practise of the poussette. Remember that this is a square formation, and the jingle for it goes: "Away from the centre, quarter turn; up or down, quarter turn; into the centre, half turn round; dance back, dance back". Each of those elements takes one bar, i.e. one Pas de Basque step. The full description is in last week's email. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/5465/
  6. Broadway (S) - We did this last week. It includes two lots of set and link for 3 couples, followed by half rights and lefts. The name "Broadway" refers to a street in New York which cuts diagonally through what is mainly a square grid of streets. This is reflected in the diagonal Rights and Lefts. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13771/
  7. The Tipsy Vicar (J) - This dance also included diagonal half rights and lefts. It finishes with a poussette. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/17360/ There is no video. 
  8. De'il Amang the Tailors (R) - An oldie but a goodie - very popular. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/1582/ It is often the last dance on a programme, as indeed it is at Carterton. It is quite boisterous and there are often "unofficial" additions or variations by experienced dancers - e.g. following the 1s down the middle, or doing a poussette while others do an allemande! (See the three videos on the bottom row!)
