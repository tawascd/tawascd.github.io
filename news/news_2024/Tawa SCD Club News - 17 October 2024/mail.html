Tonight's dances
The beginners danced numbers 1-5, after which they were able to go home. The rest of us danced no 6-8. 
  1. Antarctica Bound (J) - This was the walking warm-up dance. It is very simple, a 4C dance, and each couple get just one go.  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/11184/ 
  2. Joe MacDiarmid's Jig (J) - Apparently this dance used to get done a lot in the past, not so much these days.  This dance starts with Adv & Ret (advance and retire) across the set, and then there is Adv & Ret up and down the set. Catherine spent a little bit of time on the Adv & Ret formation. When done with partners advancing towards each other in the set, the steps have to be very small, as there are two steps forward and two steps back, andthere is not a lot of space, . In bars 9-12, the two end couples Adv towards each other from the end of the set (up and down the set) - more distance to cover, and so the steps can be a bit bigger. In the first video in the link, the set is very wide, so the steps in the Adv & Ret can be a bit longer. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/3310/
  3. Coleraine Rant (R) - Before dancing this, we had some Pas de Basque step practise, followed by practising the Poussette. More about this further down. The dance was devised by Johnsonville teacher Rod Downey, as a teaching dance for poussette. 
  4. Hope Little's Strathspey (S) - This dance includes a Grand Chain. Remembering that fitting six hands into eight bars, the "jingle" goes "right, left, ri-i-ight, left, right, le-e-eft". The 1s face each other across the set, the 2s face down and the 3s face up to start the grand chain. The dance finishes with circle six hands round. Reminder that it starts by crossing the R foot across the left, as the circle goes towards the left first. The body has to keep facing into the circle, while the legs move sideways ...! https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7420/   
  5. A Diamond for Belfast (J) - Unusually, this dance starts with a Grand chain. It also includes an Allemande for all three couples. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13852/
  6. Orpington Caledonians (R) - A great favourite, devised by Australian teacher John Brenchley. This is on the programme of the President's Ball at Summer School, at which Catherine will be the MC. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/16523/
  7. Broadway (S) - This dance includes two lots of 3C set & link, followed by half diagonal R&L. The person who did the "linking" is not involved with the half diag R&L. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13771/
  8. Gaelforce Wind (J) - This includes a 2C set& link, followed by a Men's Chain. A Men's chain works like a Ladies' Chain, except that their roles are reversed and the men cross in an S shape                    by the Left hand. This took a bit of getting used to! https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/695/ This dance is on two of the evening programmes during Summer School, including Catherine's night. Obviously, with Wellington's windy reputation and the logo of Summer School showing Wellington "blown away", this dance had to be included sooner or later. 


New learning for our new dancers
For our beginners, we had another Pas de Basque practice. Remember that the non-weight bearing foot has to be placed in front of the standing foot. This video show quite clearly how it works https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tj6iXEpQyk There is also this video from the Lower Hut club: https://youtu.be/H0_lkdhMikI.  

Then Catherine had us practice dancing a Pas de Basque moving forward and moving backwards - in couples facing each other, both hands joined. 

The Poussette was next. Just repeating her, what I wrote about the poussette in the email of 3 October.  https://youtu.be/TEXJh0Sj-K0 
Salient points:
  • Done with two couples. 
  • Couples join both hands, held slightly wider than shoulder width. Firm arms - no "spaghetti arms". 
  • It is a progression, i.e. when done, the two couples are in reversed places
  • It is danced in a square. Each couple dances half of the square: the 1s dance towards the men's side to begin, the 2s dance towards the ladies' side.
  • The "jingle" that goes with this formation is: "Away from the centre, quarter turn; up or down, quarter turn; into the centre, half turn round; dance back, dance back". Each of those elements takes one bar, i.e. one PdB step.  
  • The person who finds themselves in the corner just pivots on the spot, while their partner dances the quarter turn. 
  • You are either moving or turning, but never both at the same time.
