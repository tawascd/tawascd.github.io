Tawa SCD Club News - 7 November 2024

Hello everyone

We had good numbers tonight, 27 dancers, three-plus sets. Catherine announced that while the new dancers have been dancing five dances every club night so far, tonight they would stay for six dances, so that they will be prepared for the dances on our Final Night programme. 

It's hard to believe that we will be having our final club night next week. Where has the year gone? Here are our final club events to take note of. I hope you can attend them all.

Next week: Thursday 14 November - Our Final Club Night, 7:30 pm
The Ngaio Club will be closing their club, so that their members can attend our final night. We can also expect a sprinkling of members of other clubs to join us. We will be having supper, and we would appreciate it if members could please bring a plate of finger food to share. We will have live music from "The Cranberry Tarts", a.k.a. Aileen and Hilary, and Catherine has set a lovely programme, which is attached below, along with a crib sheet. The door charge will be "Koha". We would like to donate the proceeds of the evening to the Wellington Food Bank, as the need is so high, especially at this time of year. So to those people who have pre-paid 10-trip arrangements in place, please bring along some cash for the Koha, as we cannot transfer your pre-payment to Koha. Thank you!

Thursday 21 November, 7:30 pm - Ngaio Club Tartan and Final Night. 
Just as Ngaio members come to our final night, so we will be attending their final night. It will be at their usual venue, Khandallah Presbyterian Church Hall, Ganges Road, Khandallah (opposite the New World supermarket). Music will again be provided by Aileen and Hilary, a.k.a. "The Cranberry Tarts". This will also be a "Tartan Night", so please wear some tartan, if you can (but don't stress if you haven't any). There will be supper at the end of the night. The programme and crib sheet are attached below. 

Saturday 23 November - Tawa's end-of-year Potluck Dinner.  
This will be at John's and my place in Churton Park. Come along anytime from 4:30pm. Bring your favourite dish to share, a drink if you wish, and your partner if he/she feels like coming along. We aim to eat at 6pm, and hopefully, we will be able to do a bit of dancing on the deck between dinner and dessert. I put out a sheet tonight, for people to indicate whether they will be attending, and so far I have nine names. Please let me know if you are wanting to come. I will put out the list again next week. Or you can just email me. I would like to have an idea of how many to expect, so I can make sure I have enough plates, bowls and cutlery.

Please be careful about where you park (refer to my last weekend's email).    


Tonight's dances
  1. New Year Jig (J) - This was our walking warm-up dance. As most of you know, this dance was devised by our former tutor Maureen Robson, as a dance to teach reels to a class of children. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/17898/ It's on Tawa's Final Night programme
  2. Balmoral Strathspey (S) - This is a favourite strathspey for many. It includes reels of four along the sides - a formation that our new dancers had not yet learnt. But they learnt it very quickly and did very well. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/373/ (ignore the 2nd video, as it deviates from the instructions). On Ngaio's Final Night programme.
  3. Granville Market (J) - This is on our Final Night programme. It includes a formation that the new people had not done: "turn 1st corner RH, pass partner Right shoulder, turn 2nd corner RH, pass partner Rsh". https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13621/   On our final night. 
  4. Scott Meikle (R) - Very popular dance, we did this last week, but this was the first time for our new dancers. Catherine had us practise the tandem reels across a few times, before dancing it. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13621/ It's on next week's programme. 
  5. Hope Little's Strathspey (S) - This is not on one of the upcoming programmes, but includes a Grand Chain, which Catherine thought we should practise again, just to 'cement it' in our memories. It's the one that goes "Right, left, Ri-i-ight; Left, Right, Le-e-eft". https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7420/
  6. The Laird of Milton's Daughter (R) - On our programme next week. Another new formation for our newbies: The Corner Chain., which we practised a few times first.  https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/3659/ It's on Ngaio's Final Night programme. 
  7. The Swan and the Tay (S) - This is on Waikanae's Final Night programme. The name refers to the rivers that run through Perth in Australia (the Swan), and Perth in Scotland (the Tay). It includes "Corners pass and turn". https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/14741/
  8. City of Stirling (RT) - Also on tomorrow's Waikanae's Final Night programme. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13270/
