Tonight's dances
  1. Dame Margaret's Jig (J) - This was the "notice board" walking warm-up dance. There are no cribs or videos available online, but the quick description is: R&L; Cross, cast, 1/2 fig 8 up; adv & ret with corners; circle 6H. The dance was devised by Romaine Butterfield for Dame Margaret Sparrow, who received her honour for services to medicine and the community (particularly in the field of family planning) in 2002. She was also Senior New Zealander of the Year in 2020. Dame Margaret was a keen dancer for many decades in Kelburn and Ngaio clubs. 
  2. Toast to the Mousies (R) - This fun dance is on the programmes of both Ngaio's and our own Annual Dances. We have danced it several times at club, but a few of people had not danced it before, so Catherine took us through it slowly. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/17459/ 
  3. The Swan and the Tay (S) - The name refers to two rivers of the cities of Perth - the Swan in Perth, Australia, and the Tay in Perth, Scotland. The dance includes a formation that was new to some: "Corners pass and turn".  The dancing couple dances around the space occupied by their corners, while the corners come into the middle turn and go back to place. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/14741/ 
  4. Chased Lovers (J) - The title is a word-play on "chased" and "chaste", as the first couple don't ever touch each other. The dance includes tandem diagonal reels,  and also "corners pass and turn". https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/16465/ This is on Tawa's Annual Dance programme. 
  5. Culla Bay (S) - We did this square set strathspey last week. It is on our Annual Dance programme, so I am sure we will be doing it again a few times more before the big night. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/1473/.  
  6. Miss Johnstone of Ardrossan (R) - Devised by Roy Goldring for Muriel Johnstone, amazing SCD pianist, who hailed from Ardrossan on the North Ayrshire coast. The dance consists of two halves which are sort-of repeated, but inside out, or palindrome (or something ...). It is a 5C dance and each couple only does it once. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/9638/ This is on Ngaio's Annual Dance programme. 
  7. The Reel of the Royal Scots (R) - This was our "brief only" dance. It is very popular and one of the "must know" dances. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/5529/ Also on Ngaio's programme. 
  8.  The Rev John MacFarlane (J) - This was an extra dance, was we had time to do one more ... However, it is aa 4C dance, and we only had two 3C sets left, so it was a bit messy. Devised by NZer Gary Morris to commemorate the founding in Wellington of NZ's first Scots Church. The Rev John MacFarlane performed the first service on Petone Beach. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/5555/

NZ Summer School in Wellington 
Preparations for Wellington's hosting of the NZ Summer School (28 December 2024 to 5 January 2025) are well under way. I have received the following message from Jeanette Watson, requesting Tawa Club's help for the night of the President's Ball, on 2 January 2025. 

Hi Tawa club
We are excited to be hosting Summer School in Wellington city this year.  As this is the first time it has been in Wellington City for 17 years, we would love clubs to encourage those who may not otherwise come to these events to take part.
We are also keen to involve clubs from across the region in running the evening events. 
With this in mind, I am now approaching you to understand if Tawa club would like to support the School by providing some helpers for kitchen and door duties for the evening of the President's Ball on Thurs 2 January 2025.  For this evening, there will be some additional 'kitchen duty' time in assisting with and clearing after the President's drinks function too.
If Tawa SCD Club are able to support the School in this way, we would be very grateful.

 

The School committee recognises that those attending and helping support the evening should not be expected to pay the full door price - although we do believe they can expect to be able to dance for about half the night. We have decided to recognise this by offering clubs helping with the longer nights $90 to share among their members as appropriate.  This effectively equates to 5 people at half price.  We propose making a direct payment to the club for this however, rather than complicating things as to who pays what at the door.  
Looking forward to hearing from you.
Jeanette Watson
Member, Wellington Summer School committee

So they are looking for five volunteers. If you are able to help, please contact Jeanette beauwat@gmail.com.
