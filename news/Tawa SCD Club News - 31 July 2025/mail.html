Tawa SCD Club News - 31 July 2025

Hello everyone

Tonight was the first of our introductory classes, and we were delighted to have eight new hopefuls joining us. Existing member attendance was very good too, so we ended up with three 4C sets and one 3C set, for the first part of the class. Thank you for helping to support the new people. 


Last night's dances
We started with a warm-up and stretches in a circle, and then Catherine introduced step practice. As the beginners will be getting a separate email with the details of what they learned, I won't go into too much detail here, but Catherine covered: slip step, skip change of step, RH and LH turns, 4 hands across and back, Fig 8 across, down the middle for 3 bars, up for 3 and cast for 2.

Then she put things together into 2 dances:
  • A Jig for Jeannie (J) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/9102/ (note: it is called a "Reel for Jeannie", but Catherine used it as a jig)
  • Johnnie's New Reel (R) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/3343/ (similarly, this is called "Johnnie's New Jig", but we danced it as a reel)

After this, the new people had went home, or just watched while the rest of us danced three more dances, all of which are on the programme of the Waikanae 50th Celebration:
  • The Dundee Whaler (S) - The last 8 bars are unusual - in which the 1s "zig-zag" their way to 4th place. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/1838/ 
  • General Stuart's Reel (R) - https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/2428/ Includes a Hello-Goodbye setting. It has a star on the programme, meaning "Find a clever partner". 
  • The White Heather Jig (J) - This is a busy and very pacy dance. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/7111/ This dance also has a star.

Mobile SCD Cribs App
This is a very useful tool that can be downloaded for free onto your phone or I-pad from https://mobcrib.ca/ . It allows you to search for the cribs, diagrams and videos of dances that are on the SCD DataBase. You can make up your own list of dances, or a whole dance programme can be loaded by a club and shared with other dancers - e.g. the cribsheet for the Waikanae Dance programme gives a number (61480) which you can enter into the app. 

It pays to read the instructions on the opening page of the app, or better still, watch the instruction video. Good luck! Being a bit of a techno-klutz, I had a few hassles, but I got there in the end!
