In the new dancers' section ...
We started, as usual, with a walking warm-up dance: Lady Catherine Bruce's Reel, which we danced last week, and foot and ankle exercises. 

We reviewed stepping up or down, which we learned last week:  "Step, cross, step close".  

Followed by a dance we did last week called "All Right", because all the formations use the Right hand (RH). It goes like this (using abbreviations):
1-8 1s cross RH, cast down, 2s step up;  1s cross RH, cast up, 2s step down 
9-16  2s cross RH, cast up, 1s step down; 2s cross RH, cast down,1s step up
17-24 1s lead down for 4 bars, back up for 4 bars
25-32 1s and 2s dance allemande 

Step practise
We reviewed the Strathspey travelling step. Keep the feet turned out, so that they meet in a "T" shape when the back foot closes up to the front foot. Step, close, step, hop.

Dancing strathspey in a circle: The steps are travelling steps, which usually move you forward. In a circle, however, you have to move sideways. In a circle, one usually circles to the left first, but one always starts with the right foot. So the right foot has to cross to the left to begin the circle. The trick here is that though your feet are going sideways, your body should still be facing into the circle. https://youtu.be/L_3BgigdQO4 

Dancing a chain 
  • Starting in a circle, couples face each other.
  • Of each couple, one will travel around the circle clockwise, the other will go anti-clockwise.
  • Change places with the person you are facing by the Right hand (RH), but keep facing the way you are going
  • Change places with the next person LH, keep travelling in the same direction
  • Repeat: RH, LH, RH, LH. 
  • Each "hand" takes two bars (two steps) 

Next we formed four-couple sets, and danced a chain, as above. The "chain" is in the shape of a rectangle.
  • Starting with the 1s facing each other, across the set; the 2s facing the 3s on the sides; the 4s facing each other across the set.
  • Dance a chain - RH, LH, repeat four times (16 bars)
  • Keep going in the same direction, to get back to where you started from. 

Next we danced the same chain, but in groups of two couples. The "chain" is in the shape of a square. 
  • 1s and 2s face their partners across the set. 
  • Change places RH with partner across the set
  • Change places LH with the other person along the side
  • Repeat: RH across, then LH on the sides, to get back to where you started from. 

And here we have arrived at a new formation called "Rights and Lefts"
  • In each couple, one of you will be going clockwise, the other anti-clockwise. 
  • Across is always right hand;  along the side is always left hand
  • The hands should stay at above waist level - do not let them droop! 
  • When you have done the whole formation, you are back in the same position from where you started. 
  • In the video you will see that two of the people in each square finish with what is called a "courtesy turn". Don't worry about that just yet. Catherine will cover that in due course.
Having learned "Rights and Lefts", we then danced "Rakes of Glasgow", a Strathspey, which starts with Rights and Lefts (abbreviation: R&L) 

We also danced "Crabbit Shona", which is a jig, and finishes with R&L.

  • Overview: provides the name of the deviser, the type of dance and number of couples, and formations
  • Extra info: sometimes has interesting details about the dance
  • Crib/Diagrams: description of the dance. Uses standard abbreviations (a glossary of abbreviations is attached below)
  • Videos: popular dances have lots of videos, not all of them excellent. Look for the ones that are "demonstration" quality. 
  • You can ignore the other tabs for now.


Tonight's dances
  1. Lady Catherine Bruce's Reel (J) - This was our walking warm-up dance. We danced it last week. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/3594/
  2. All Right (R) - We did this last week too. It's the one that uses the right hand each time. Full description was in last week's new dancers' email. 
  3. Rakes of Glasgow (S) - This dance starts with Right and Lefts, and finishes with a circle. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/5462/
  4. Bonjour Au Revoir (R) - For the more experienced. This dance was devised by Gaye Collin of the Lower Hutt club. The name is French for "Hello-Goodbye", as it includes a "Hello-Goodbye" setting. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/17228/ 
  5. Crabbit Shona (J) - This dance finishes with Rights and Lefts. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/13259/ The video looks a bit confusing. There are three 2-couple sets, two on stage, and one on the floor. The 1s are closest to the audience. 
  6. Jean Martin of Aberdeen (S) - This dance includes a 2C Bourrel. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/12542/ It is on the programme of the Lower Hutt dance. 
  7. The Ullapool Ferry (R) - This dance is also on Lower Hutt's programme. It has two stars, indicating that it is a bit tricky. https://my.strathspey.org/dd/dance/18409/ It includes a "Hello-Goodbye Poussette", which is a formation devised by Rod Downey, of the Johnsonville Club. It is a blend of Hello-Goodbye setting, with a poussette. I won't try to describe it here. I looked up the description on the SCD Dictionary, but it makes it sound very complicated. Catherine explained it very well - I liked her description of the people "in the middle" dancing HG setting, while holding a "basket" (= their partners) who just follow along. The Ullapool Ferry sails daily between Ullapool in Wester Ross and Stornoway on the Isle of Lewis. Here's a photo of the Ullapool Ferry, taken by Joy Tracey.
