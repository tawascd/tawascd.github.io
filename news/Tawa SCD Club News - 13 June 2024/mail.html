Tawa SCD Club News - 13 June 2024

Hello everyone

What a wonderful night’s dancing we had tonight! We had 33 dancers, including visitors. It was lovely to see Dallas - formerly our club president, and now resident in Hastings. She was down in Wellington for work, and stayed an extra day just so she could come and dance with us. Unfortunately she won’t be able to make it down again for our Annual Dance. We had live music from Hilary and Jason - with Jason playing accordion, in Aileen’s absence - what a talented multi-instrument musician he is! We usually see him playing the keyboard or the tuba (for Trip to Bavaria). But did you know that he also plays the clarinet, the alto and tenor saxophones, the ukulele, and the pipe organ?

Dances and their stars
  • The cribsheet that Catherine put out is attached again. The stars indicate the level of difficulty. 
  • The dances will not be walked. So if you have any doubts about being able to do a dance from just a briefing, you may want to enjoy watching the dance from the sidelines. 
  • You might want to print off the cribsheet, and decide ahead of time, which dances you will be able to do, and which ones you will sit out. 
  • A 2-star dance means:  Find a clever partner who can help you.
  • The safe place to be if you are a little unsure, is in 4th position, so you can watch the first time through. It is OK to ask another couple to swap places with you so you can be in 4th place. 
  • A 3-star dance means: This is an advanced dance, don’t get up to dance it unless you know it. If you get up anyway and muck it up, it will be disappointing for the other people in the set. 
  • It is OK to politely decline an invitation to dance, especially if it’s a 3-star dance that you do not know.
